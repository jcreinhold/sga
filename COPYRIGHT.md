# Copyright and rights

This repository contains an English translation, in Markdown with Unicode mathematics, of the Grothendieck-era
*Séminaires de Géométrie Algébrique du Bois-Marie* (SGA). Two layers of rights apply.

## Underlying French text

The SGA seminars were published in various forms between 1960 and the modern SMF *Documents Mathématiques* re-editions.
Authors and editors include A. Grothendieck, M. Raynaud, M. Demazure, P. Berthelot, L. Illusie, N. Katz, J.-P. Serre,
J.-L. Verdier, and others. Publishers include Springer (Lecture Notes in Mathematics — e.g. LNM 224 for SGA 1) and the
Société Mathématique de France (*Documents Mathématiques* series, edited by M. Raynaud, Y. Laszlo, M. Demazure, and
others). The French source text used here was drawn from the Grothendieck Circle archive
(<https://webusers.imj-prg.fr/~leila.schneps/grothendieckcircle/pubtexts.php>).

The original text is © the original authors and publishers, all rights reserved. This translation is offered as a
non-commercial scholarly aid under *fair use* / *usage loyal* provisions for scholarship and education. No grant of
rights in the underlying French text is made or implied. If you hold rights and have concerns, please open an issue
or contact Jacob Reinhold.

### LLM-assisted production

This translation was produced with substantial assistance from large language models and then curated and edited by
Jacob Reinhold. Copyright is claimed on the human contribution: terminology decisions, glossing policy, structural
arrangement, post-editing of model output, and per-volume conventions. The copyright status of passages that are
predominantly raw model output varies by jurisdiction:

- **United States and European Union.** Purely AI-generated material is not copyrightable; copyright attaches only to
    the human contribution (*Thaler v. Perlmutter*, 2023; CJEU *Infopaq*, 2009).
- **United Kingdom.** Computer-generated works lacking a human author carry a 50-year copyright vesting in "the person
    by whom the arrangements necessary for the creation of the work are undertaken" (CDPA 1988 §9(3)).
- **China.** Case-by-case; recent Beijing Internet Court decisions have granted copyright where the user demonstrated
    meaningful creative input through prompt iteration and post-editing.
