**Notations.** вҖ” We denote by `QвӮҠ` the set of positive (or zero) rational numbers; one has `в„Ө вҲ© QвӮҠ = в„•`. Let `V` be a
`в„ҡ`-vector space; if `A` (resp. `B`) is a subset of `в„ҡ` (resp. `V`), we denote by `A В· B` the image of `A вҠ— B` under the
morphism `в„ҡ вҠ— V вҶ’ V`, in other words the set of linear combinations of elements of `B` with coefficients in `A`. We

## 1. Generalities

<!-- label: III.XXI.1 -->

### 1.1. Definitions, first properties

<!-- label: III.XXI.1.1 -->

**Definition 1.1.1.** *Let `M` and `M*` be two free `в„Ө`-modules of finite type in duality. Write `V = M вҠ— в„ҡ`,
`V* = M* вҠ— в„ҡ`; these are two `в„ҡ`-vector spaces in duality. We identify `M` (resp. `M*`) with a subset of `V`
(resp. `V*`). The canonical bilinear form on `M* Г— M` (resp. `V* Г— V`) is denoted `( , )`.*

<!-- label: III.XXI.1.1.1 -->

<!-- original page 86 -->

*Let `R` be a finite subset of `M`. Suppose we are given a map `Оұ вҶҰ Оұ*` of `R` into `M*`; the set of `Оұ*`, for `Оұ вҲҲ R`,
is denoted `R*`. To each `Оұ вҲҲ R` we associate the endomorphism `s_Оұ` (resp. `s*_Оұ`) of `M` and `V` (resp. `M*` and
`V*`) given by the formulas:*

```text
(1)    s_Оұ(x) = x вҲ’ (Оұ*, x)Оұ,    i.e.   s_Оұ = id вҲ’ Оұ* вҠ— Оұ;
(1*)   s*_Оұ(u) = u вҲ’ (u, Оұ)Оұ*,   i.e.   s*_Оұ = id вҲ’ Оұ вҠ— Оұ*.
```

*One says that the pair `(R, R*)` (more precisely the pair `(R, R вҶ’ M*)`) is a* root datum *in `(M, M*)`, or that
`(M, M*, R, R*)` is a* root datum, *if the following axioms are satisfied:*

```text
(DR I)   For each Оұ вҲҲ R, one has    (Оұ*, Оұ) = 2.
(DR II)  For each Оұ вҲҲ R, one has    s_Оұ(R) вҠӮ R,    s*_Оұ(R*) вҠӮ R*.
```

*One says that `R` is the* root system *of the root datum `в„ӣ = (M, M*, R, R*)`. The elements of `R` (resp. `R*`) are
called the* roots *(resp.* coroots*) of the root datum.*

**Remark 1.1.2.** *Axiom (DR I) is equivalent to any of the following properties:*

<!-- label: III.XXI.1.1.2 -->

```text
(2)   s_Оұ s_Оұ = id,        (2*)  s*_Оұ s*_Оұ = id,
(3)   s_Оұ(Оұ) = вҲ’Оұ,         (3*)  s*_Оұ(Оұ*) = вҲ’Оұ*.
```

**Remark 1.1.3.** *Axioms (DR I) and (DR II) entail*

<!-- label: III.XXI.1.1.3 -->

```text
R = вҲ’R,   R* = вҲ’R*,   0 вҲү R,   0 вҲү R*.
```

<!-- original page 87 -->

**Lemma 1.1.4.** *The map `R вҶ’ R*` is a bijection. More generally, if `Оұ, ОІ вҲҲ R` and `(Оұ*, x) = (ОІ*, x)` for all
`x вҲҲ R`, then `Оұ = ОІ`.*

<!-- label: III.XXI.1.1.4 -->

*Proof.* Indeed, one then has `s_ОІ(Оұ) = Оұ вҲ’ 2ОІ`, `s_Оұ(ОІ) = ОІ вҲ’ 2Оұ`. One deduces at once

```text
s_ОІ s_Оұ(Оұ) = 2ОІ вҲ’ Оұ = Оұ + 2(ОІ вҲ’ Оұ),    s_ОІ s_Оұ(ОІ вҲ’ Оұ) = s_ОІ(ОІ вҲ’ Оұ) = ОІ вҲ’ Оұ,
```

whence `(s_ОІ s_Оұ)вҒҝ(Оұ) = Оұ + 2n(ОІ вҲ’ Оұ) вҲҲ R` by (DR II). Since `R` is finite, one has `ОІ вҲ’ Оұ = 0`.

**Corollary 1.1.5.** *The inverse map `R* вҶ’ R` defines a root datum*

<!-- label: III.XXI.1.1.5 -->

```text
в„ӣ* = (M*, M, R*, R)
```

*called the* dual *of `в„ӣ`.*[^N.D.E-XXI-2]

**Definition 1.1.6.** *We denote by `О“вӮҖ(R)` the subgroup of `M` generated by `R`. We denote by `V(R)` the vector
subspace of `V` generated by `R`, that is to say `О“вӮҖ(R) вҠ— в„ҡ`. Applying these definitions to `в„ӣ*`, one constructs
similarly `О“вӮҖ(R*)` and `V(R*)`.*

<!-- label: III.XXI.1.1.6 -->

*One calls* reductive rank *of `в„ӣ` the number*

```text
rgred(в„ӣ) = rank(M) = dim(V) = dim(V*) = rank(M*) = rgred(в„ӣ*).
```

*One calls* semisimple rank *of `в„ӣ` the number*

```text
rgss(в„ӣ) = rank(R) = rank(О“вӮҖ(R)) = dim(V(R)).
```

*One thus has `rgss(в„ӣ) в©Ҫ rgred(в„ӣ)`.*

*We shall see below that `rgss(в„ӣ) = rgss(в„ӣ*)`, that is to say that `V(R)` and `V(R*)` have the same dimension.*

<!-- original page 88 -->

**Definition 1.1.7.** *One says that `в„ӣ` is* semisimple *(resp.* trivial*) if `rgss(в„ӣ) = rgred(в„ӣ)` (resp.
`rgss(в„ӣ) = 0`). For `в„ӣ` to be trivial it is therefore necessary and sufficient that `R` be empty. The trivial root
datum of reductive rank zero is denoted `0 = ({0}, {0}, вҲ…, вҲ…)`.*

<!-- label: III.XXI.1.1.7 -->

**Definition 1.1.8.** *We denote by `W(в„ӣ)` the group of transformations of `M` generated by the `s_Оұ`, `Оұ вҲҲ R`. We
call it the* Weyl group *of `в„ӣ`. We write*

<!-- label: III.XXI.1.1.8 -->

```text
W*(в„ӣ) = W(в„ӣ*).
```

*Then `W(в„ӣ)` operates on `R`, `О“вӮҖ(R)`, `V(R)`, `M` and `V`. If `w вҲҲ W(в„ӣ)` and `x вҲҲ M` (resp. `x вҲҲ V`), one has
`wx вҲ’ x вҲҲ О“вӮҖ(R)` (resp. `wx вҲ’ x вҲҲ V(R)`); this is immediate from formula (1). Likewise for `W*(в„ӣ)`.*

**Lemma 1.1.9.** *For all `Оұ вҲҲ R`, `x вҲҲ V`, `u вҲҲ V*`, one has*

<!-- label: III.XXI.1.1.9 -->

```text
(4)    (s*_Оұ(u), s_Оұ(x)) = (u, x).
```

*Proof.* Indeed, taking the scalar product of (1) and (1*), one finds that the left-hand side equals
`(u, x) + (u, Оұ)(Оұ*, x)((Оұ*, Оұ) вҲ’ 2) = (u, x)`.

**Remark 1.1.10.** *If one assumes `0 вҲү R` and `0 вҲү R*`, then 1.1.9 is equivalent to (DR I).*

<!-- label: III.XXI.1.1.10 -->

**Corollary 1.1.11.** *Denote by `h вҶҰ h^вҲЁ` the isomorphism of `GL(M)` onto `GL(M*)` that associates to `h` its
contragredient. Then formula (4) is also written*

<!-- label: III.XXI.1.1.11 -->

```text
(5)    (s_Оұ)^вҲЁ = s*_Оұ.
```

**Corollary 1.1.12.** *The preceding isomorphism induces an isomorphism of `W(в„ӣ)` onto `W*(в„ӣ)`.*

<!-- label: III.XXI.1.1.12 -->

<!-- original page 89 -->

**Scholie 1.1.13.** *By virtue of the preceding result, we shall identify `W` and `W*`, and we shall regard `W` as a
group of transformations of `R`, `R*`, `M`, `M*`, `О“вӮҖ(R)`, `О“вӮҖ(R*)`, `V`, `V*`, `V(R)`, `V(R*)`. We shall write `s_Оұ`
for `s*_Оұ`.*

<!-- label: III.XXI.1.1.13 -->

### 1.2. The map `p`

<!-- label: III.XXI.1.2 -->

**Lemma 1.2.1.** *Let `p : M вҶ’ M*` (resp. `V вҶ’ V*`) be the linear map defined by*

<!-- label: III.XXI.1.2.1 -->

```text
(6)    p(x) = ОЈ_{u вҲҲ R*} (u, x) u.
```

*Write `в„“(x) = (p(x), x)`. One has the following properties:*

```text
(7)    в„“(x) в©ҫ 0,    в„“(Оұ) > 0    for Оұ вҲҲ R.

(8)    в„“(w x) = в„“(x),    for w вҲҲ W.

(9)    (p(x), y) = (p(y), x),    for x, y вҲҲ V.

(10)   в„“(Оұ) Оұ* = 2 p(Оұ),    for Оұ вҲҲ R.
```

*Proof.* The first three relations are immediate.[^N.D.E-XXI-3] Let us prove the last. By (1), for `u вҲҲ V*`:

```text
(u, Оұ)ВІ Оұ* = (u, Оұ) u вҲ’ (u, Оұ) s_Оұ(u)
           = (u, Оұ) u + (u, s_Оұ(Оұ)) s_Оұ(u)
           = (u, Оұ) u + (s_Оұ(u), Оұ) s_Оұ(u)    (since s_ОұВІ = id).
```

<!-- original page 90 -->

Since `s_Оұ` is a permutation of `R*` (by (DR II)), it remains only to sum over `u вҲҲ R*` to conclude.

**Scholie 1.2.2.** *Relation (10) says that `Оұ вҶҰ в„“(Оұ) Оұ*` is the restriction to `R` of a linear map from `M` into
`M*`. In particular, one has*

<!-- label: III.XXI.1.2.2 -->

```text
(вҲ’Оұ)* = вҲ’Оұ*.
```

**Corollary 1.2.3.** *The map `p` induces an isomorphism of `V(R)` onto `V(R)*`.*

<!-- label: III.XXI.1.2.3 -->

*Proof.* Indeed, `p` sends `V(R)` into `V(R*)`. We thus have

```text
dim(V(R)) в©ҫ dim(V(R*)).
```

Applying this inequality to the dual root datum, we deduce

```text
dim(V(R)) = dim(V(R*)),
```

so `p`, being surjective, is also bijective.

**Corollary 1.2.4.** *One has `dim(V(R)) = dim(V(R*))`, hence*

<!-- label: III.XXI.1.2.4 -->

```text
rgss(в„ӣ) = rgss(в„ӣ*).
```

**Corollary 1.2.5.** *The bilinear form `(u, x)` is nondegenerate on `V(R*) Г— V(R)`, and so puts these `в„ҡ`-vector
spaces in duality.*

<!-- label: III.XXI.1.2.5 -->

*Proof.* Indeed, if `(u, x) = 0` for all `u вҲҲ R*`, then `p(x) = 0`.

**Corollary 1.2.6.** *The symmetric bilinear form `(p(x), y)` is positive nondegenerate on `V(R)`.*

<!-- label: III.XXI.1.2.6 -->

<!-- original page 91 -->

**Corollary 1.2.7.** *`W` operates faithfully on `R` (and hence on the other sets of 1.1.13).*

<!-- label: III.XXI.1.2.7 -->

*Proof.* Indeed, let `u вҲҲ V*`. Let `w вҲҲ W`; suppose that `w(Оұ) = Оұ` for all `Оұ вҲҲ R`, and let us prove that `w(u) = u`.
One has

```text
(w(u) вҲ’ u, Оұ) = (w(u), Оұ) вҲ’ (u, Оұ) = (u, wвҒ»В№(Оұ)) вҲ’ (u, Оұ) = 0.
```

But `w(u) вҲ’ u вҲҲ V(R*)`. If it is orthogonal to all the roots, it is zero by 1.2.5.

**Corollary 1.2.8.** *The group `W` is finite.*

<!-- label: III.XXI.1.2.8 -->

**Proposition 1.2.9.** *The operations of `W` respect the correspondence between roots and coroots. In other words,
for `Оұ вҲҲ R` and `w вҲҲ W`, one has*

<!-- label: III.XXI.1.2.9 -->

```text
w(Оұ*) = w(Оұ)*.
```

*Proof.* It suffices to verify this for `w = s_ОІ`, `ОІ вҲҲ R`, that is to say to verify the formula

```text
s_ОІ(Оұ*) = s_ОІ(Оұ)*.
```

Now `в„“(s_ОІ(Оұ)) В· s_ОІ(Оұ)* / 2` equals:

```text
p(s_ОІ(Оұ)) = ОЈ_{u вҲҲ R*} (u, s_ОІ(Оұ)) u = ОЈ_{u вҲҲ R*} (s_ОІ(u), Оұ) u = ОЈ_{u вҲҲ R*} (u, Оұ) s_ОІ(u) = s_ОІ(p(Оұ));
```

since `в„“(s_ОІ(Оұ)) = в„“(Оұ)`, one obtains `s_ОІ(Оұ)* = s_ОІ(2 p(Оұ) / в„“(Оұ)) = s_ОІ(Оұ*)`.

<!-- original page 92 -->

**Corollary 1.2.10.** *If `Оұ вҲҲ R` and `w вҲҲ W`, one has*

<!-- label: III.XXI.1.2.10 -->

```text
w s_Оұ wвҒ»В№ = s_{w(Оұ)}.
```

*Proof.* Indeed, `w s_Оұ wвҒ»В№(x) = x вҲ’ (Оұ*, wвҒ»В№(x)) w(Оұ) = x вҲ’ (w(Оұ*), x) w(Оұ)`, and this last term equals, by 1.2.9:

```text
x вҲ’ (w(Оұ)*, x) w(Оұ) = s_{w(Оұ)}(x).
```

**Corollary 1.2.11.** *Let `R' вҠӮ R`. For `(M, M*, R', R'*)` to be a root datum, it is necessary and sufficient that
`Оұ, ОІ вҲҲ R'` imply `s_Оұ(ОІ) вҲҲ R'`.*

<!-- label: III.XXI.1.2.11 -->

## 2. Relations between two roots

<!-- label: III.XXI.2 -->

### 2.1. Proportional roots

<!-- label: III.XXI.2.1 -->

**Proposition 2.1.1.** *Let `Оұ` and `ОІ` be two roots. The following conditions are equivalent:*

<!-- label: III.XXI.2.1.1 -->

*(i) There exists `k вҲҲ в„ҡ` such that `Оұ = k ОІ`.*

*(ii) `s_Оұ = s_ОІ`.*

*Moreover, under these conditions one has `Оұ* = kвҒ»В№ ОІ*`, and `k` is equal to one of the numbers `1`, `вҲ’1`, `2`, `вҲ’2`,
`1/2`, `вҲ’1/2`.*

*Proof.* Suppose first (i). One has first

```text
Оұ* = в„“(Оұ)вҒ»В№ В· 2 p(Оұ) = kвҒ»ВІ в„“(ОІ)вҒ»В№ В· 2 k p(ОІ) = kвҒ»В№ ОІ*.
```

This entails at once `s_Оұ = s_ОІ`. Conversely, if `s_Оұ = s_ОІ`, then

```text
Оұ = s_Оұ(вҲ’Оұ) = s_ОІ(вҲ’Оұ) = (ОІ*, Оұ) ОІ вҲ’ Оұ,
```

whence `2 Оұ = (ОІ*, Оұ) ОІ`, with `(ОІ*, Оұ) вҲҲ в„Ө`, so (ii) entails (i). Finally, if `Оұ = k ОІ`, then `Оұ* = kвҒ»В№ ОІ*`, whence

```text
(Оұ*, ОІ) = 2 kвҒ»В№,    (ОІ*, Оұ) = 2 k,
```

so `2k` and `2kвҒ»В№` are integers and we are done.

<!-- original page 93 -->

**Application 2.1.2.** *The root data `в„ӣ` with `rgss(в„ӣ) = 1` are of one of the following two types:*

<!-- label: III.XXI.2.1.2 -->

*(i)* Type `AвӮҒ`: *there exists `Оұ вҲҲ M` such that the roots are `Оұ` and `вҲ’Оұ`. The coroots are then `Оұ*` and `вҲ’Оұ*`.*

*(ii)* Type `AвӮҒ'`: *there exists `Оұ вҲҲ M` such that the roots are `Оұ`, `вҲ’Оұ`, `2Оұ`, `вҲ’2Оұ`. The coroots are then `Оұ*`,
`вҲ’Оұ*`, `Оұ*/2`, `вҲ’Оұ*/2`.*

**Definition 2.1.3.** *One says that `Оұ вҲҲ R` is* indivisible *if `Оұ/2 вҲү R`. One says that `в„ӣ` is* reduced *if every
root is indivisible.*

<!-- label: III.XXI.2.1.3 -->

*For `в„ӣ` to be reduced it is necessary and sufficient that `в„ӣ*` be so. If `Оұ` is indivisible and if `w вҲҲ W`, then
`w(Оұ)` is indivisible.*

**Definition 2.1.4.** *Let `Оұ вҲҲ R`. If `Оұ` is indivisible, set `ind(Оұ) = Оұ`. Otherwise, set `ind(Оұ) = Оұ/2`.*

<!-- label: III.XXI.2.1.4 -->

**Corollary 2.1.5.** *If `Оұ вҲҲ R` is indivisible and if `k Оұ вҲҲ R`, where `k вҲҲ в„ҡ`, then `k вҲҲ в„Ө`.*

<!-- label: III.XXI.2.1.5 -->

**Proposition 2.1.6.** *Let `в„ӣ` be a root datum. Then*

<!-- label: III.XXI.2.1.6 -->

```text
ind(в„ӣ) = (M, M*, ind(R), ind(R)*)
```

*is a reduced root datum, and one has*

```text
W(ind(в„ӣ)) = W(в„ӣ).
```

*Proof.* Indeed, `ind(в„ӣ)` is a root datum by 1.2.11, since the Weyl group permutes indivisible roots. The second
assertion follows from 2.1.1.

<!-- original page 94 -->

**Remark 2.1.7.** *If `в„ӣ` is not reduced, one has `ind(R)* вү  ind(R*)` and so `ind(в„ӣ*) вү  ind(в„ӣ)*`.*

<!-- label: III.XXI.2.1.7 -->

### 2.2. Orthogonal roots

<!-- label: III.XXI.2.2 -->

**Lemma 2.2.1.** *Let `Оұ` and `ОІ` be two roots. One has*

<!-- label: III.XXI.2.2.1 -->

```text
(11)    в„“(Оұ) (Оұ*, ОІ) = в„“(ОІ) (ОІ*, Оұ).
```

*Proof.* This follows at once from 1.2.1, formulas (9) and (10).

**Corollary 2.2.2.** *Let `Оұ, ОІ вҲҲ R`. The following conditions are equivalent:*

<!-- label: III.XXI.2.2.2 -->

*(i) `(Оұ*, ОІ) = 0`,    (i bis) `(ОІ*, Оұ) = 0`,*

*(ii) `(p(Оұ), ОІ) = 0`,*

*(iii) `s_Оұ(ОІ) = ОІ`,    (iii bis) `s_ОІ(Оұ) = Оұ`,*

*(iv) `s_Оұ(ОІ*) = ОІ*`,    (iv bis) `s_ОІ(Оұ*) = Оұ*`,*

*(v) `s_Оұ вү  s_ОІ` and `s_Оұ` and `s_ОІ` commute.*

*Proof.* All the equivalences are immediate, except those involving (v). Let us show that (i) (and (i bis)) entail
(v). One has

```text
s_Оұ s_ОІ(x) = x вҲ’ (ОІ*, x) ОІ вҲ’ (Оұ*, x) Оұ + (ОІ*, x) (Оұ*, ОІ) Оұ.
```

If `(Оұ*, ОІ) = 0`, then `(ОІ*, Оұ) = 0` and `s_Оұ s_ОІ(x) = s_ОІ s_Оұ(x)`.

Suppose conversely (v). One has

```text
s_Оұ = s_ОІ s_Оұ s_ОІ = s_{s_ОІ(Оұ)}    (by 1.2.10).
```

By 2.1, there exists `k вҲҲ в„ҡ` such that `Оұ = k s_ОІ(Оұ) = k Оұ вҲ’ k (ОІ*, Оұ) ОІ`. Since `s_Оұ вү  s_ОІ`, `ОІ` and `Оұ` are not
proportional by 2.1.1, hence `(ОІ*, Оұ) = 0`.

**Definition 2.2.3.** *Two roots satisfying the equivalent conditions of 2.2.2 are called* orthogonal.

<!-- label: III.XXI.2.2.3 -->

<!-- original page 95 -->

**Remark 2.2.4.** *The roots `Оұ` and `ОІ` are orthogonal if and only if the coroots `Оұ*` and `ОІ*` are orthogonal.*

<!-- label: III.XXI.2.2.4 -->

**Lemma 2.2.5.** *If `Оұ` and `ОІ` are two orthogonal roots, then `Оұ + ОІ вҲҲ R` if and only if `Оұ вҲ’ ОІ вҲҲ R`.*

<!-- label: III.XXI.2.2.5 -->

*Proof.* Indeed, `s_ОІ(Оұ + ОІ) = s_ОІ(Оұ) + s_ОІ(ОІ) = Оұ вҲ’ ОІ`.

**Lemma 2.2.6.** *Let `Оұ` and `ОІ` be two non-orthogonal roots. If one defines `в„“(Оі*)` for a coroot `Оі*` as `в„“` of the
root `Оі*` of `в„ӣ*`, one has the relation*

<!-- label: III.XXI.2.2.6 -->

```text
(12)    в„“(Оұ) в„“(Оұ*) = в„“(ОІ) в„“(ОІ*).
```

*Proof.* Indeed, multiplying formula (11) by the corresponding formula for `в„ӣ*`, and using the equality `(Оі*)* = Оі`
for all `Оі вҲҲ R`, one finds:

```text
(ОІ*, Оұ) (Оұ*, ОІ) в„“(Оұ) в„“(Оұ*) = (ОІ*, Оұ) (Оұ*, ОІ) в„“(ОІ) в„“(ОІ*).
```

### 2.3. General case

<!-- label: III.XXI.2.3 -->

**Proposition 2.3.1.** *If `Оұ` and `ОІ` are any two roots, one has*

<!-- label: III.XXI.2.3.1 -->

```text
(13)    0 в©Ҫ (Оұ*, ОІ)(ОІ*, Оұ) в©Ҫ 4.
```

*If `Оұ` and `ОІ` are neither proportional nor orthogonal, one has*

```text
1 в©Ҫ (Оұ*, ОІ)(ОІ*, Оұ) в©Ҫ 3.
```

*Proof.* Indeed, one has `4 (p(Оұ), ОІ)ВІ = в„“(Оұ) в„“(ОІ) (Оұ*, ОІ)(ОІ*, Оұ)`. On the other hand, by 1.2.6 the symmetric bilinear
form `(p(x), y)` is positive nondegenerate on `V(R)`, whence `(p(x), y)ВІ в©Ҫ в„“(x) в„“(y)`.[^N.D.E-XXI-4]

<!-- original page 96 -->

**Corollary 2.3.2.** *Let `Оұ` and `ОІ` be two non-orthogonal roots. If `в„“(Оұ) = в„“(ОІ)`, there exists `w вҲҲ W` such that
`w(Оұ) = ОІ`.*

<!-- label: III.XXI.2.3.2 -->

*Proof.* Indeed, if `Оұ` and `ОІ` are proportional, then since `в„“(Оұ) = в„“(ОІ)`, one has `Оұ = ОІ` or `Оұ = вҲ’ОІ`; in this case
one takes `w = 1` or `w = s_Оұ`. If `Оұ` and `ОІ` are neither proportional nor orthogonal, then by formula (11) and
2.3.1:

```text
(ОІ*, Оұ) = (Оұ*, ОІ) = Вұ1.
```

If `(ОІ*, Оұ) = (Оұ*, ОІ) = 1`, one takes `w = s_ОІ s_Оұ s_ОІ`. If `(ОІ*, Оұ) = (Оұ*, ОІ) = вҲ’1`, one takes `w = s_Оұ s_ОІ`.

**Corollary 2.3.3.** *If `Оұ` and `ОІ` are two roots, if `Оұ вү  ОІ` and `(ОІ*, Оұ) > 0` (resp. if `Оұ вү  вҲ’ОІ` and `(ОІ*, Оұ) < 0`),
then `Оұ вҲ’ ОІ` (resp. `Оұ + ОІ`) is a root.*

<!-- label: III.XXI.2.3.3 -->

*Proof.* The second case is deduced from the first by changing `ОІ` into `вҲ’ОІ`. If `Оұ` and `ОІ` are proportional and
`(ОІ*, Оұ) > 0`, then `ОІ = Оұ`, `2ОІ = Оұ`, or `ОІ = 2Оұ`. The first case is excluded. In the others, one has respectively
`Оұ вҲ’ ОІ = ОІ вҲҲ R` or `Оұ вҲ’ ОІ = вҲ’Оұ вҲҲ R`. If `Оұ` and `ОІ` are not proportional, `(Оұ*, ОІ)` and `(ОІ*, Оұ)` are two strictly
positive integers whose product is at most `3`. One of them is therefore equal to `1`. If `(ОІ*, Оұ) = 1`, one has
`Оұ вҲ’ ОІ = s_ОІ(Оұ) вҲҲ R`; if `(Оұ*, ОІ) = 1`, one has `Оұ вҲ’ ОІ = вҲ’s_Оұ(ОІ) вҲҲ R`.

**Lemma 2.3.4.** *Let `Оұ` and `ОІ` be two non-proportional roots. If `ОІ вҲ’ Оұ` is not a root, then `ОІ + k Оұ вҲҲ R` for
`k = вҲ’(Оұ*, ОІ)`, but not for `k = вҲ’(Оұ*, ОІ) + 1`.*

<!-- label: III.XXI.2.3.4 -->

*Proof.* Indeed, `ОІ вҲ’ (Оұ*, ОІ) Оұ = s_Оұ(ОІ) вҲҲ R`, but `ОІ + (вҲ’(Оұ*, ОІ) + 1) Оұ = s_Оұ(ОІ вҲ’ Оұ) вҲү R`.

**Proposition 2.3.5.** *Let `Оұ` and `ОІ` be two non-proportional roots. The set of integers `k` such that `ОІ + k Оұ вҲҲ R`
is an interval `[p, q]` (`p в©Ҫ 0`, `q в©ҫ 0`), and one has `p + q = вҲ’(Оұ*, ОІ)`.*

<!-- label: III.XXI.2.3.5 -->

<!-- original page 97 -->

*Proof.* For the first assertion it suffices, for example, to prove that if `ОІ + k Оұ вҲҲ R`, `k` integer `> 0`, then
`ОІ + (k вҲ’ 1) Оұ вҲҲ R`. If `k = 1`, this is trivial. If `k в©ҫ 2`, one has

```text
(Оұ*, ОІ + k Оұ) = (Оұ*, ОІ) + 2k в©ҫ вҲ’3 + 4 > 0,
```

and one concludes by 2.3.3. Let then `[p, q]` be the interval in question. Applying 2.3.4 to `ОІ + p Оұ`, one finds

```text
q вҲ’ p = вҲ’(Оұ*, ОІ + p Оұ) = вҲ’(Оұ*, ОІ) вҲ’ 2p.
```

[^N.D.E-XXI-5]

**Remark 2.3.6.** *The previous formula contains the qualitative statements 2.2.5 and 2.3.3.*

<!-- label: III.XXI.2.3.6 -->

**Complements 2.3.7.**[^N.D.E-XXI-6] *By 1.2.1 (9) and 1.2.6, the bilinear form on `V(R)` defined by*

<!-- label: III.XXI.2.3.7 -->

```text
вҹЁx, yвҹ© = (p(x), y)
```

*is symmetric and positive definite. By 1.2.1 (10), for any `Оұ вҲҲ R` and `y вҲҲ V(R)`,*

```text
вҹЁОұ, yвҹ© = (в„“(Оұ)/2) (Оұ*, y),
```

*where `в„“(Оұ) = вҹЁОұ, Оұвҹ©`. Consequently, one deduces from 2.3.3 the following corollary.*

**Corollary.** *Let `Оұ вү  ОІ` in `R`. If `Оұ вҲ’ ОІ вҲү R`, then `вҹЁОұ, ОІвҹ© в©Ҫ 0`.*

## 3. Simple roots, positive roots

<!-- label: III.XXI.3 -->

### 3.1. Systems of simple roots

<!-- label: III.XXI.3.1 -->

**Lemma 3.1.1.** *Let `Оұ` and `Оұбөў`, `i = 1, вҖҰ, n`, be roots. Assume `Оұ` is distinct from the `Оұбөў`. If one has a relation*

<!-- label: III.XXI.3.1.1 -->

```text
Оұ = ОЈбөў qбөў Оұбөў,    qбөў вҲҲ QвӮҠ,
```

*there exists an index `i` such that `qбөў вү  0`, `(Оұ*, Оұбөў) > 0`, and `Оұ вҲ’ Оұбөў вҲҲ R`.*

*Proof.* Indeed, one writes `2 = (Оұ*, Оұ) = ОЈбөў qбөў (Оұ*, Оұбөў)`, which proves the first two assertions. The third then
follows from 2.3.3.

**Proposition 3.1.2.** *Let `Оұ` and `Оұбөў`, `i = 1, вҖҰ, n`, be roots. If*

<!-- label: III.XXI.3.1.2 -->

```text
Оұ = ОЈбөў mбөў Оұбөў,    mбөў вҲҲ в„•вӮҠ,
```

<!-- original page 98 -->

*there exists a sequence `ОІвӮҒ, вҖҰ, ОІ_m` of roots taken from the `Оұбөў` (not necessarily pairwise distinct) such that if one
denotes*

```text
Оі_p = ОЈ_{i=1}^{p} ОІбөў,    p = 1, вҖҰ, m,
```

*one has `Оі_p вҲҲ R` and `Оі_m = Оұ`.*

*Proof.* We argue by induction on the integer `ОЈ mбөў = m'`. If `Оұ` equals one of the `Оұбөў`, say `Оұ_{iвӮҖ}` (which is
automatic if `m' = 1`), one takes `m = 1`, `ОІвӮҒ = Оұ_{iвӮҖ}`. Otherwise, one applies the previous lemma and there exists
an index `i` such that `mбөў вү  0` and `Оұ вҲ’ Оұбөў` is a root. One then has `(mбөў вҲ’ 1) вҲҲ в„•` and

```text
Оұ вҲ’ Оұбөў = (mбөў вҲ’ 1) Оұбөў + ОЈ_{j вү  i} mвұј Оұвұј.
```

It remains only to apply the induction hypothesis to `Оұ вҲ’ Оұбөў`.

**Corollary 3.1.3.** *Let `R' вҠӮ R`. The following conditions are equivalent:*

<!-- label: III.XXI.3.1.3 -->

*(i) If `Оұ, ОІ вҲҲ R'` and `Оұ + ОІ вҲҲ R`, then `Оұ + ОІ вҲҲ R'`.*

*(ii) `(в„• В· R') вҲ© R вҠӮ R'`.*

*Proof.* Indeed, one has clearly (ii) вҮ’ (i). The converse follows at once from the proposition.

**Definition 3.1.4.** *A set of roots satisfying the conditions of 3.1.3 is called* closed.

<!-- label: III.XXI.3.1.4 -->

**Proposition 3.1.5.** *Let `О” вҠӮ R` be a set of roots. The following conditions are equivalent:*

<!-- label: III.XXI.3.1.5 -->

*(i) The elements of `О”` are indivisible, linearly independent, and*

<!-- original page 99 -->

```text
R вҠӮ (QвӮҠ В· О”) вҲӘ (вҲ’QвӮҠ В· О”).
```

*(ii) The elements of `О”` are linearly independent and*

```text
R вҠӮ (в„• В· О”) вҲӘ (вҲ’в„• В· О”).
```

*(iii) Every root is written in a unique way as a linear combination of the elements of `О”`, with integer
coefficients all of the same sign.*

[^N.D.E-XXI-7] One has obviously (ii) вҮ’ (iii). Denote by `ОұвӮҒ, вҖҰ, Оұ_n` the (distinct) elements of `О”`.

*Proof of* (i) вҮ’ (ii). Let `Оұ вҲҲ R`. One has thus a unique expression

```text
Оө Оұ = ОЈбөў qбөў Оұбөў    qбөў вҲҲ QвӮҠ, Оө = Вұ1.
```

Let us show that the `qбөў` are integers. This is certainly true if all but one of them are zero (cf. 2.1.5). Otherwise,
`Оұ` is distinct from the `Оұбөў` and, applying 3.1.1, one finds `iвӮҖ` such that `Оұ' = Оө Оұ вҲ’ Оұ_{iвӮҖ} вҲҲ R` and `q_{iвӮҖ} вү  0`.
This gives

```text
Оұ' = (q_{iвӮҖ} вҲ’ 1) Оұ_{iвӮҖ} + ОЈ_{i вү  iвӮҖ} qбөў Оұбөў.
```

<!-- original page 100 -->

Since at least one of the `qбөў`, `i вү  iвӮҖ`, is nonzero, (i) entails `q_{iвӮҖ} вҲ’ 1 в©ҫ 0`. One repeats the operation for `Оұ'`
and after finitely many steps one has shown that the `qбөў` are integers.

*Proof of* (iii) вҮ’ (i). Let us show that `ОұвӮҒ, ОұвӮӮ, вҖҰ, Оұ_n` are linearly independent over `в„ҡ`. In the contrary case, one
would have an equality

```text
x = ОЈ_{i вҲҲ I} aбөў Оұбөў = ОЈ_{j вҲҲ J} bвұј Оұвұј,
```

where `I`, `J` are two disjoint subsets of `{1, вҖҰ, n}`, at least one of them, say `I`, being nonempty, and
`aбөў, bвұј вҲҲ в„•*`. By Corollary 2.3.7, one has `вҹЁОұбөў, Оұвұјвҹ© в©Ҫ 0` if `i вҲҲ I` and `j вҲҲ J`, whence `вҹЁx, xвҹ© в©Ҫ 0` and so `x = 0`.
Let `iвӮҖ вҲҲ I`; then the equalities

```text
Оұ_{iвӮҖ} = Оұ_{iвӮҖ} + ОЈ_{i вҲҲ I} aбөў Оұбөў = Оұ_{iвӮҖ} + ОЈ_{j вҲҲ J} bвұј Оұвұј
```

entail (by (iii)) `aбөў = 0 = bвұј` for all `i`, `j`, a contradiction. This shows that the elements of `О”` are linearly
independent over `в„ҡ`; let us show they are also indivisible.

So let `ОІ вҲҲ О”` be divisible. One has `ОІ/2 вҲҲ R`, whence

```text
ОІ/2 = Оө ОЈ_{Оұ вҲҲ О”} m_Оұ Оұ,    m_Оұ вҲҲ в„•, Оө = Вұ1,
```

so also `ОІ = 2 Оө ОЈ_Оұ m_Оұ Оұ`, whence by uniqueness `m_Оұ = 0` if `Оұ вү  ОІ` and `2 Оө m_ОІ = 1`, a contradiction.

**Definition 3.1.6.** *A set `О”` of roots satisfying the conditions of 3.1.5 is called a* system of simple roots, *or a*
base of `в„ӣ`.

<!-- label: III.XXI.3.1.6 -->

*If `w вҲҲ W` and if `О”` is a system of simple roots, then `w(О”)` is a system of simple roots.*

**Remark 3.1.7.** *This definition involves only `R` and not `в„ӣ`, and in fact involves only `ind(R)`.*

<!-- label: III.XXI.3.1.7 -->

**Remark 3.1.8.** *If `О”` is a system of simple roots, `О”` is a basis of the free abelian group `О“вӮҖ(R)`. One has thus
`Card(О”) = rgss(в„ӣ)`.*

<!-- label: III.XXI.3.1.8 -->

**Remark 3.1.9.** *The conditions of 3.1.5 are then evidently equivalent to:*

<!-- label: III.XXI.3.1.9 -->

*(iвҖІ) The elements of `О”` are indivisible, of number `в©Ҫ rgss(в„ӣ)`, and `R вҠӮ (QвӮҠ В· О”) вҲӘ (вҲ’QвӮҠ В· О”)`.*

*(iiвҖІ) `Card(О”) в©Ҫ rgss(в„ӣ)` and `R вҠӮ (в„• В· О”) вҲӘ (вҲ’в„• В· О”)`.*

<!-- original page 101 -->

**Corollary 3.1.10.** *If `О”` is a system of simple roots, then `ind(О”*)` is a system of simple coroots (i.e. a system
of simple roots of `в„ӣ*`).*

<!-- label: III.XXI.3.1.10 -->

*Proof.* Indeed, if `ОІ = ОЈ_{Оұ вҲҲ О”} a_Оұ Оұ` (`a_Оұ вҲҲ в„•`), then `p(ОІ) = ОЈ_{Оұ вҲҲ О”} a_Оұ p(Оұ)`, whence, by 1.2.1 (10):

```text
ОІ* = ОЈ_{Оұ вҲҲ О”} a_Оұ (в„“(Оұ)/в„“(ОІ)) Оұ*,
```

which shows that `ind(О”*)` satisfies (iвҖІ).

[^N.D.E-XXI-8] By 2.1.1, if `Оұ*` is not indivisible, then `Оұ* = 2 u*`, where `u* вҲҲ ind(О”*)` (and `u = 2Оұ`). One
deduces:

**Corollary 3.1.11.** *If `О”` is a system of simple roots and if `ОІ = ОЈ_{Оұ вҲҲ О”} a_Оұ Оұ` (`a_Оұ вҲҲ в„Ө`) is the expression
of `ОІ` along `О”`, then `2 a_Оұ в„“(Оұ)` is divisible by `в„“(ОІ)` (and even by `2 в„“(ОІ)` if `Оұ*` is indivisible).*

<!-- label: III.XXI.3.1.11 -->

Before continuing to state the properties of systems of simple roots, let us show that such systems exist.

### 3.2. Systems of positive roots

<!-- label: III.XXI.3.2 -->

**Definition 3.2.1.** *A subset `RвӮҠ вҠӮ R` is called a* system of positive roots *of `R` (or of `в„ӣ`, cf. Remark 3.2.2) if
it satisfies the following conditions:*

<!-- label: III.XXI.3.2.1 -->

*(P 1) `RвӮҠ вҲ© вҲ’RвӮҠ = вҲ…`.*

*(P 2) `RвӮҠ вҲӘ вҲ’RвӮҠ = R`.*

*(P 3) `(QвӮҠ В· RвӮҠ) вҲ© R вҠӮ RвӮҠ`.*

*In particular, such a set is closed. We shall see later (3.3.8) that in fact a closed subset satisfying (P 1) and
(P 2) also satisfies (P 3), and hence is a system of positive roots. If `w вҲҲ W` and if `RвӮҠ` is a system of positive
roots, then `w(RвӮҠ)` is a system of positive roots.*

<!-- original page 102 -->

**Remark 3.2.2.** *This definition involves only `R`. One will also say that `RвӮҠ` is a system of positive roots of `в„ӣ`.*

<!-- label: III.XXI.3.2.2 -->

**Remark 3.2.3.** *From (P 1) and (P 2), one obtains at once*

<!-- label: III.XXI.3.2.3 -->

```text
Card(RвӮҠ) = Card(R)/2.
```

*It follows that if `RвӮҠ` and `R'вӮҠ` are two systems of positive roots with `RвӮҠ вҠӮ R'вӮҠ`, then `RвӮҠ = R'вӮҠ`.*

**Remark 3.2.4.** *If `RвӮҠ` is a system of positive roots, `R*вӮҠ` is a system of positive coroots (i.e. a system of
positive roots of `в„ӣ*`).*

<!-- label: III.XXI.3.2.4 -->

*This follows at once from 1.1.4 and 1.2.2.*

**Definition 3.2.5.** *Let `О”` be a system of simple roots. Set*

<!-- label: III.XXI.3.2.5 -->

```text
P(О”) = (QвӮҠ В· О”) вҲ© R = (в„•вӮҠ В· О”) вҲ© R.
```

**Proposition 3.2.6.** *If `О”` is a system of simple roots, `P(О”)` is a system of positive roots. If `О”` is a system of
simple roots and `RвӮҠ` is a system of positive roots, one has the equivalence:*

<!-- label: III.XXI.3.2.6 -->

```text
О” вҠӮ RвӮҠ  вҹә  RвӮҠ = P(О”).
```

*Proof.* The first assertion is immediate. If `О” вҠӮ RвӮҠ`, then `P(О”) вҠӮ RвӮҠ` by (P 3), so `P(О”) = RвӮҠ` by 3.2.3. The rest is
trivial.

**Remark 3.2.7.** *Systems of positive roots exist: let `в©ҫ` be a structure of totally ordered vector space on `V(R)`.
The set of roots `в©ҫ 0` for this order relation is a system of positive roots.*

<!-- label: III.XXI.3.2.7 -->

<!-- original page 103 -->

**Theorem 3.2.8.** *Let `RвӮҠ` be a system of positive roots. There exists a unique system of simple roots `рқ’®(RвӮҠ)` such
that `рқ’®(RвӮҠ) вҠӮ RвӮҠ`, i.e. such that `RвӮҠ = P(рқ’®(RвӮҠ))`.*

<!-- label: III.XXI.3.2.8 -->

*Proof.* Uniqueness follows at once from:

**Lemma 3.2.9.** *Let `О”` be a system of simple roots. Then for `Оұ вҲҲ P(О”)` to belong to `О”`, it is necessary and
sufficient that `Оұ` not be a sum of two elements of `P(О”)`.*

<!-- label: III.XXI.3.2.9 -->

*Proof.* This lemma follows at once from the definitions and from 3.1.2.

Let us now prove the existence of `рқ’®(RвӮҠ)`. Consider the set of subsets `T` of `RвӮҠ` such that `T = ind(T)` and
`(QвӮҠ В· T) вҠғ RвӮҠ`. This set is nonempty, since it contains `ind(RвӮҠ)`. Let `О”` be a minimal element of this set for the
inclusion relation. We show that `О”` is a system of simple roots, that is to say, by 3.1.5 (i), that `О”` is a free
subset of `M вҠ— в„ҡ`.

**Lemma 3.2.10.** *If `Оұ, ОІ вҲҲ О”` and `q Оұ + q' ОІ вҲҲ R`, where `q, q' вҲҲ в„ҡ`, then `q q' в©ҫ 0`.*

<!-- label: III.XXI.3.2.10 -->

*Proof.* Indeed, if `q q' < 0`, one can write (possibly exchanging `Оұ` and `ОІ`)

```text
a Оұ вҲ’ b ОІ вҲҲ RвӮҠ,    a, b вҲҲ Q*вӮҠ,
```

so by hypothesis there exists a relation

```text
a Оұ вҲ’ b ОІ = ОЈ_{Оі вҲҲ О”} c(Оі) Оі,    c(Оі) вҲҲ QвӮҠ.
```

If `a в©Ҫ c(Оұ)`, it is written

```text
вҲ’ОІ = ((c(Оұ) вҲ’ a)/b) Оұ + ОЈ_{Оі вү  Оұ} (c(Оі)/b) Оі.
```

Then this element belongs to `(QвӮҠ В· О”) вҲ© R`, which is contained in `RвӮҠ` by (P 3). But then `ОІ вҲҲ RвӮҠ вҲ© вҲ’RвӮҠ`, which
contradicts (P 1).

If, on the contrary, `a > c(Оұ)`, one writes

```text
(a вҲ’ c(Оұ)) Оұ = b ОІ + ОЈ_{Оі вү  Оұ} c(Оі) Оі,
```

<!-- original page 104 -->

which proves `О” вҠӮ QвӮҠ В· (О” вҲ’ {Оұ})`, contrary to the minimal character of `О”`.

Recall (cf. 2.3.7) that the bilinear form on `V(R)` defined by `вҹЁx, yвҹ© = (p(x), y)` is a Euclidean inner product;
moreover, for `Оұ вҲҲ R` one has `вҹЁОұ, yвҹ© = в„“(Оұ) (Оұ*, y) / 2`, so that `вҹЁОұ, yвҹ©` and `(Оұ*, y)` have the same sign.

**Lemma 3.2.11.** *If `Оұ, ОІ вҲҲ О”`, then `(ОІ*, Оұ) в©Ҫ 0` and so `вҹЁОІ, Оұвҹ© в©Ҫ 0`.*

<!-- label: III.XXI.3.2.11 -->

*Proof.* Indeed, `s_ОІ(Оұ) = Оұ вҲ’ (ОІ*, Оұ) ОІ вҲҲ R`, whence `(ОІ*, Оұ) в©Ҫ 0` by 3.2.10.

Let us now prove that `О”` is free. In the contrary case, 3.2.11 entails, as in the proof of 3.1.5, the existence of a
non-trivial relation

```text
ОЈ_{Оұ вҲҲ О”} m(Оұ) Оұ = 0,    m(Оұ) вҲҲ в„•,
```

whence `вҲ’ОұвӮҖ = (m(ОұвӮҖ) вҲ’ 1) ОұвӮҖ + ОЈ_{Оұ вү  ОұвӮҖ} m(Оұ) Оұ`, if `m(ОұвӮҖ) в©ҫ 1`. Then, by (P 3), `ОұвӮҖ` belongs to `RвӮҠ вҲ© вҲ’RвӮҠ`,
contradicting (P 1).

This shows that `О”` is a base of `в„ӣ` and completes the proof of Theorem 3.2.8.

**Corollary 3.2.12.** *Let `RвӮҠ` be a system of positive roots, `О”` a base of `в„ӣ`, and `w вҲҲ W`. One has:*

<!-- label: III.XXI.3.2.12 -->

```text
О” вҠӮ RвӮҠ  вҹә  RвӮҠ = P(О”)  вҹә  О” = рқ’®(RвӮҠ)

P(ind(О”*)) = P(О”)*,    рқ’®(R*вӮҠ) = ind(рқ’®(RвӮҠ)*);

рқ’®(w(RвӮҠ)) = w(рқ’®(RвӮҠ)),    P(w(О”)) = w(P(О”)).
```

<!-- original page 105 -->

**Definition 3.2.13.** *If one has chosen a system of simple roots `О”`, the elements of `P(О”)` will be called*
positive. *If one has chosen a system of positive roots `RвӮҠ`, the elements of `рқ’®(RвӮҠ)` will be called* simple.

<!-- label: III.XXI.3.2.13 -->

**Corollary 3.2.14.** *Let `RвӮҠ` be a system of positive roots. Let `Оұ вҲҲ RвӮҠ`. The following conditions are equivalent:*

<!-- label: III.XXI.3.2.14 -->

*(i) `Оұ` is simple (i.e. `Оұ вҲҲ рқ’®(RвӮҠ)`).*

*(ii) `Оұ` is not a sum of two elements of `RвӮҠ`.*

*(iii) `RвӮҠ вҲ’ {Оұ}` is closed (cf. 3.1.4).*

*Proof.* The equivalence of (i) and (ii) follows at once from 3.2.9. The equivalence of (ii) and (iii) is immediate.

**Definition 3.2.15.** *Let `О”` be a system of simple roots. The sum of the coefficients of the decomposition of a root
`Оұ` along `О”` is called the* order of `Оұ` *relative to `О”` and is denoted `ord_О”(Оұ)`.*

<!-- label: III.XXI.3.2.15 -->

*One has the equivalences:*

```text
Оұ вҲҲ P(О”)  вҹә  ord_О”(Оұ) > 0,    Оұ вҲҲ О”  вҹә  ord_О”(Оұ) = 1.
```

<!-- original page 106 -->

**Lemma 3.2.16.** *Let `О”` be a system of simple roots and `Оұ вҲҲ P(О”)`. There exists a sequence `Оұбөў вҲҲ О”`, `i = 1, вҖҰ, m`,
such that*

<!-- label: III.XXI.3.2.16 -->

```text
ОұвӮҒ + ОұвӮӮ + вӢҜ + Оұ_p вҲҲ P(О”)    for p = 1, вҖҰ, m,
ОұвӮҒ + ОұвӮӮ + вӢҜ + Оұ_m = Оұ.
```

*Moreover, for every such sequence `Оұбөў`, one has `m = ord_О”(Оұ)`.*

*Proof.* Trivial by 3.1.2.

### 3.3. Characterization and conjugacy of systems of positive roots

<!-- label: III.XXI.3.3 -->

**Lemma 3.3.1.** *If `Оұ вҲҲ P(О”)`, `ОІ вҲҲ О”`, and if `Оұ` and `ОІ` are not proportional, then `s_ОІ(Оұ) вҲҲ P(О”)`.*

<!-- label: III.XXI.3.3.1 -->

*Proof.* Indeed, `s_ОІ(Оұ) = Оұ вҲ’ (ОІ*, Оұ) ОІ`. Since at least one simple root other than `ОІ` appears in the decomposition
of `Оұ` with a nonzero (hence strictly positive) coefficient, it also appears in the decomposition of `s_ОІ(Оұ)` with the
same coefficient, so `s_ОІ(Оұ)` is also positive.

**Corollary 3.3.2.** *If `ОІ вҲҲ О”`, the symmetry `s_ОІ` interchanges the elements of `P(О”)` not proportional to `ОІ`.*

<!-- label: III.XXI.3.3.2 -->

**Lemma 3.3.3.** *If `Оұ вҲҲ P(О”) вҲ’ О”`, `Оұ` indivisible, there exists `ОІ вҲҲ О”` such that `s_ОІ(Оұ) вҲҲ P(О”)` and
`ord_О”(s_ОІ(Оұ)) < ord_О”(Оұ)`.*

<!-- label: III.XXI.3.3.3 -->

*Proof.* Indeed, by 3.1.1 there exists `ОІ вҲҲ О”` such that `(ОІ*, Оұ) > 0`. Since `Оұ вҲү О”` and is indivisible, `Оұ` cannot be
proportional to `ОІ`. Hence `s_ОІ(Оұ) вҲҲ P(О”)`, by 3.3.1, and one has
`ord_О”(s_ОІ(Оұ)) = ord_О”(Оұ) вҲ’ (ОІ*, Оұ) < ord_О”(Оұ)`.

**Corollary 3.3.4.** *If `Оұ вҲҲ P(О”)` is indivisible, there exists a sequence `ОІ_p вҲҲ О”`, `p = 1, вҖҰ, q`, such that*

<!-- label: III.XXI.3.3.4 -->

```text
Оұ_p = s_{ОІ_p} вӢҜ s_{ОІ_1}(Оұ) вҲҲ P(О”)    for p = 1, вҖҰ, q,
```

*and `Оұ_q вҲҲ О”`.*

*Proof.* This follows from 3.3.3 by induction on `ord_О”(Оұ)`.

**Proposition 3.3.5.** *The Weyl group is generated by the `s_Оұ`, for `Оұ вҲҲ О”`. Every indivisible root is conjugate to
a simple root by an element of the Weyl group.*

<!-- label: III.XXI.3.3.5 -->

<!-- original page 107 -->

*Proof.* The second assertion follows at once from 3.3.4. The first follows from it by 1.2.10 and 2.1.1.

**Proposition 3.3.6.** *Let `RвӮҠ` be a system of positive roots. Let `P' вҠӮ R` satisfy (P 2) and be closed. Then there
exists `w вҲҲ W` such that `w(RвӮҠ) вҠӮ P'`.*

<!-- label: III.XXI.3.3.6 -->

Let us state the corollaries at once.

**Corollary 3.3.7.** *The Weyl group operates transitively on the set of systems of positive roots (resp. of systems of
simple roots).*

<!-- label: III.XXI.3.3.7 -->

**Corollary 3.3.8.** *For a subset of `R` to be a system of positive roots, it is necessary and sufficient that it
satisfy (P 1) and (P 2) and be closed.*

<!-- label: III.XXI.3.3.8 -->

**Corollary 3.3.9.** *If one endows `О“вӮҖ(R)` with a structure of ordered group such that every root is `> 0` or `< 0`,
the set of positive roots for this order structure is a system of positive roots.*

<!-- label: III.XXI.3.3.9 -->

*Proof of 3.3.6.* One can find `w вҲҲ W` such that `Card(w(RвӮҠ) вҲ© P')` is maximal, hence by replacing `RвӮҠ` by `w(RвӮҠ)` one
may assume that

```text
(вҲ—)    Card(RвӮҠ вҲ© P') в©ҫ Card(RвӮҠ вҲ© s_Оұ(P'))
```

for all `Оұ вҲҲ рқ’®(RвӮҠ) = О”`. Let us show that `RвӮҠ вҠӮ P'`. Otherwise, `P'` being closed, there exists `Оұ вҲҲ О”`, `Оұ вҲү P'`. But
`P'` satisfying (P 2), one has then `вҲ’Оұ вҲҲ P'` (so `вҲ’2Оұ вҲҲ P'` if `2Оұ` is a root). For every `ОІ вҲҲ RвӮҠ вҲ© P'`, one has
`ОІ вү  Оұ`; if `2Оұ` is not a root, then `s_Оұ(ОІ) вҲҲ RвӮҠ` (by 3.3.1), so

```text
s_Оұ(RвӮҠ вҲ© P') вҠӮ RвӮҠ вҲ© s_Оұ(P');
```

but `RвӮҠ вҲ© s_Оұ(P')` also contains `Оұ = s_Оұ(вҲ’Оұ)`, contradicting inequality (вҲ—). If `2Оұ` is a root, one argues similarly.

To study sets of roots satisfying (P 2) and closed, one is therefore reduced to the case where they contain a set of
positive roots.

**Proposition 3.3.10.** *Let `RвӮҠ` be a system of positive roots and `P'` a closed subset of `R` containing `RвӮҠ`. If one
denotes `О” = рқ’®(RвӮҠ)` and `О”' = О” вҲ© вҲ’P'`, then `P'` is the union of `RвӮҠ` and the set of roots that are linear
combinations with negative coefficients of the elements of `О”'`.*

<!-- label: III.XXI.3.3.10 -->

<!-- original page 108 -->

*Proof.* We prove the assertion by induction on the order of a root `Оі вҲҲ P' вҲ’ RвӮҠ`. If `ord_О”(Оі) = вҲ’1`, then `вҲ’Оі вҲҲ О”`
and `Оі вҲҲ вҲ’О”'`. If `ord_О”(Оі) < вҲ’1`, there exists, by 3.1.2, `ОІ вҲҲ О”` such that `вҲ’Оі вҲ’ ОІ вҲҲ R`; then

```text
0 < ord_О”(вҲ’Оі вҲ’ ОІ) = ord_О”(вҲ’Оі) вҲ’ 1 < ord_О”(вҲ’Оі)
```

and the first inequality shows that `вҲ’Оі вҲ’ ОІ вҲҲ RвӮҠ`. Hence, since `RвӮҠ вҲ© вҲ’RвӮҠ = вҲ…` and `Оі + ОІ` is a sum of two roots of
`P'`, it is an element of `P' вҲ’ RвӮҠ` with `ord_О”(Оі + ОІ) > ord_О”(Оі)`. So, by the induction hypothesis, `Оі + ОІ` is a
linear combination with negative coefficients of the elements of `О”'`. Since `Оі = (Оі + ОІ) вҲ’ ОІ`, it suffices to verify
that `ОІ вҲҲ вҲ’P'`. Now `Оі вҲҲ P'` and `вҲ’(Оі + ОІ) вҲҲ RвӮҠ вҠӮ P'`, so `вҲ’ОІ = Оі вҲ’ (Оі + ОІ)` belongs to `P'`.

**Definition 3.3.10.1.**[^N.D.E-XXI-9] *One says that a subset `R'` of `R` is* symmetric *if `вҲ’R' = R'`.*

<!-- label: III.XXI.3.3.10.1 -->

**Scholie 3.3.11.** *Let `P'` be a set of roots satisfying (P 2) and closed. There exists a system of simple roots `О”`
and a subset `О”'` of `О”` such that*

<!-- label: III.XXI.3.3.11 -->

```text
P' = R вҲ© (в„• В· О” вҲӘ вҲ’в„• В· О”').
```

*If one denotes `R_{О”'} = (в„Ө В· О”') вҲ© R`, which is a closed and symmetric subset of `R`, then `P'` is the disjoint
union of `R_{О”'}` and of the closed part of `P(О”)` formed by the `Оұ вҲҲ P(О”) вҲ’ в„• В· О”'`, i.e. the positive roots which
in the decomposition along `О”` "contain at least one root of `О” вҲ’ О”'`".*

### 3.4. Closed and symmetric sets of roots

<!-- label: III.XXI.3.4 -->

**Proposition 3.4.1.** *Let `в„ӣ = (M, M*, R, R*)` be a root datum and `R'` a closed and symmetric subset of `R`. Then:*

<!-- label: III.XXI.3.4.1 -->

*(i) `в„ӣ' = (M, M*, R', R'*)` is a root datum;*

<!-- original page 109 -->

*(ii) for every system of positive roots `RвӮҠ` of `R`, `R'вӮҠ = RвӮҠ вҲ© R'` is a system of positive roots of `R'`;*

*(iii) the Weyl group `W(в„ӣ')` of `в„ӣ'` is the subgroup of `W(в„ӣ)` generated by the `s_Оұ`, for `Оұ вҲҲ R'`.*

*Proof.* The first assertion is trivial by 1.2.11, the second follows from 3.3.8, the third is evident.

**Corollary 3.4.2.** *Let `w вҲҲ W(в„ӣ')`. The order of `w` is the smallest integer `n > 0` such that `wвҒҝ(Оұ') = Оұ'` for
every `Оұ' вҲҲ R'`.*

<!-- label: III.XXI.3.4.2 -->

*Proof.* It suffices to apply 1.2.7 to the root datum `в„ӣ'`.

**Corollary 3.4.3.** *Let `Оұ` and `ОІ` be two non-proportional roots. Let `n` be the smallest integer `> 0` such that
`(s_Оұ s_ОІ)вҒҝ(Оұ) = Оұ` and `(s_Оұ s_ОІ)вҒҝ(ОІ) = ОІ`. Then the subgroup `W_{Оұ,ОІ}` of `W` generated by `s_Оұ` and `s_ОІ` is
defined by the relations:*

<!-- label: III.XXI.3.4.3 -->

```text
s_ОұВІ = 1,    s_ОІВІ = 1,    (s_Оұ s_ОІ)вҒҝ = 1.
```

*Proof.* Taking 3.4.2 into account, it suffices to verify:

**Lemma 3.4.4.** *Let `G` be the group generated by two elements `x` and `y` subject to the relations `xВІ = yВІ = 1`.
Every normal subgroup of `G` containing neither `x` nor `y` is generated (as a normal subgroup) by an element of the
form `(xy)вҒҝ`, where `n > 0`.*

<!-- label: III.XXI.3.4.4 -->

[^N.D.E-XXI-10] *Proof.* Indeed, every element of `G` is written `(xy)вҒҝ`, or `(yx)вҒҝ = (xy)вҒ»вҒҝ`, or:

```text
(a)    x (yx)^{2n}    or    y (xy)^{2n+1}

(b)    y (xy)^{2n}    or    x (yx)^{2n+1},
```

where `n вҲҲ в„•`. Now the elements of type (a) (resp. (b)) are conjugate to `x` (resp. to `y`).

**Remark 3.4.5.** *One computes the integer `n` immediately: if one sets*

<!-- label: III.XXI.3.4.5 -->

```text
(Оұ*, ОІ) = p,    (ОІ*, Оұ) = q,
```

<!-- original page 110 -->

*one has[^N.D.E-XXI-11]*

```text
(s_Оұ s_ОІ)(Оұ) = (pq вҲ’ 1) Оұ вҲ’ q ОІ,    (s_Оұ s_ОІ)(ОІ) = p Оұ вҲ’ ОІ.
```

*The integer `n` is therefore the order of the matrix*

```text
вҺӣpq вҲ’ 1   pвҺһ
вҺқ  вҲ’q   вҲ’1вҺ .
```

*If `pq = 0`, then `p = q = 0`, by 2.2.2, whence `n = 2`. Otherwise, by 2.3.1, `pq` equals `1`, `2`, or `3`, and one
finds, respectively, `n = 3`, `4`, or `6`.*

*N.B. By writing that the order of the previous matrix is finite, one recovers inequality (13) of 2.3.1.*

**Definition 3.4.6.** *Let `О”` be a system of simple roots and `О”' вҠӮ О”`. We write*

<!-- label: III.XXI.3.4.6 -->

```text
R_{О”'} = R вҲ© (в„ҡ В· О”') = R вҲ© (в„Ө В· О”').
```

**Lemma 3.4.7.** *`R_{О”'}` is closed and symmetric, `О”'` is a system of simple roots of the root datum
`(M, M*, R_{О”'}, R*_{О”'})`, whose Weyl group is the subgroup `W_{О”'}` of `W` generated by the `s_Оұ`, for `Оұ вҲҲ О”'`. One
has `О” вҲ© R_{О”'} = О”'`.*

<!-- label: III.XXI.3.4.7 -->

*Proof.* Trivial.

**Proposition 3.4.8.** *Let `R' вҠӮ R`. The following conditions are equivalent:*

<!-- label: III.XXI.3.4.8 -->

*(i) There exists a vector subspace `V'` of `V` (or of `V(R)`) such that `R' = R вҲ© V'`.*

*(ii) There exists a system of simple roots `О”` of `R` and a subset `О”'` of `О”` such that `R' = R_{О”'}`.*

*More precisely, under these conditions, every system of simple roots `О”'` of `(M, M*, R', R'*)` is contained in a
system of simple roots `О”` of `R`, and one has `R' = R_{О”'}`.*

<!-- original page 111 -->

*Proof.* One has obviously (ii) вҮ’ (i). Suppose (i) verified: then `R'` is closed and symmetric, so
`(M, M*, R', R'*)` is a root datum. Let `О”'` be a system of simple roots of this datum and `R'вӮҠ = P(О”')`. If `V' = V`,
then `О”'` is a system of simple roots of `в„ӣ` and we are done. Otherwise, there exists `x вҲҲ V*` such that

```text
(x, V') = {0},    (x, Оұ) вү  0    for all Оұ вҲҲ R вҲ’ R'.
```

Put `RвӮҠ(x) = {Оұ вҲҲ R | (x, Оұ) > 0}` and `RвӮҠ = RвӮҠ(x) вҲӘ R'вӮҠ`. For every `Оұ вҲҲ R`, one has the equivalences

```text
(x, Оұ) > 0  вҹә  Оұ вҲҲ RвӮҠ(x),
(x, Оұ) < 0  вҹә  Оұ вҲҲ вҲ’RвӮҠ(x),
(x, Оұ) = 0  вҹә  Оұ вҲҲ R'.
```

It follows at once from 3.3.8 that `RвӮҠ` is a system of positive roots of `R`. Put `О” = рқ’®(RвӮҠ)`. It evidently suffices
to prove `О”' вҠӮ О”`. Otherwise let `Оұ вҲҲ О”' вҲ’ О”`. Then, by 3.2.14, there exist `ОІ, Оі вҲҲ RвӮҠ` such that `Оұ = ОІ + Оі`. If
`ОІ, Оі вҲҲ RвӮҠ(x)`, one has `Оұ вҲҲ RвӮҠ(x)`, which is absurd since `(x, О”') = 0`. If `ОІ` or `Оі`, say `ОІ`, belongs to `R'вӮҠ`,
then, since `R'` is symmetric and closed, `Оі = Оұ вҲ’ ОІ` belongs to `RвӮҠ вҲ© R' = R'вӮҠ`; but then `Оұ` is not simple in `R'вӮҠ`.

**Lemma 3.4.9.** *Under the preceding conditions, let `Оұ вҲҲ P(О”) вҲ’ R'`. For every `w вҲҲ W_{О”'}`, one has
`w(Оұ) вҲҲ P(О”) вҲ’ R'`.*

<!-- label: III.XXI.3.4.9 -->

<!-- original page 112 -->

*Proof.* It indeed suffices to verify this for `w = s_ОІ`, `ОІ вҲҲ О”'`, in which case it follows from 3.3.1 and from the
fact that `s_ОІ(R') = R'`.

**Lemma 3.4.10.** *Let `w вҲҲ W`. Under the conditions of 3.4.8, the following conditions are equivalent:*

<!-- label: III.XXI.3.4.10 -->

*(i) `w вҲҲ W_{О”'}`.*

*(ii) For every `m вҲҲ M`, `w(m) вҲ’ m вҲҲ V'`.*

*(iii) For every `Оұ вҲҲ R`, `w(Оұ) вҲ’ Оұ вҲҲ V'`.*

*Proof.* One has obviously (i) вҮ’ (ii) вҮ’ (iii). Let us prove (iii) вҮ’ (i). Let then `w вҲҲ W` be such that `w(Оұ) вҲ’ Оұ вҲҲ V'`
for all `Оұ вҲҲ R`. Write `w = s_{Оұ_n} вӢҜ s_{Оұ_1}`, with `Оұбөў вҲҲ О”`, and let us prove by induction on `n` that each
`Оұбөў вҲҲ О”'`. One may assume that `w' = s_{Оұ_{nвҲ’1}} вӢҜ s_{Оұ_1} вҲҲ W_{О”'}`. One has then, for all `Оұ вҲҲ R`,

```text
w(Оұ) вҲ’ Оұ = w'(Оұ) вҲ’ Оұ вҲ’ (Оұ*_n, w'(Оұ)) Оұ_n.
```

Taking `Оұ = w'вҒ»В№(Оұ_n)`, one finds `2 Оұ_n вҲҲ V'`, whence `Оұ_n вҲҲ О”'`, so `w = s_{Оұ_n} w'` belongs to `W_{О”'}`.

### 3.5. Miscellaneous remarks

**Proposition 3.5.1.** *Let `RвӮҠ` be a system of positive roots. Denote*

<!-- label: III.XXI.3.5.1 -->

```text
ПҒ_{RвӮҠ} = (1/2) ОЈ_{Оұ вҲҲ ind(RвӮҠ)} Оұ.
```

*Then `(ОІ*, ПҒ_{RвӮҠ}) = 1` for every `ОІ вҲҲ рқ’®(RвӮҠ)`.*

```text
2 ПҒ_{RвӮҠ} = ОІ + ОЈ_{Оұ вҲҲ ind(RвӮҠ), Оұ вү  ОІ} Оұ,
```

so `s_ОІ(2 ПҒ_{RвӮҠ}) = 2 ПҒ_{RвӮҠ} вҲ’ 2ОІ`, by 3.3.2.

**Corollary 3.5.2.** *Put `ПҒ*_{RвӮҠ} = (1/2) ОЈ_{Оұ* вҲҲ ind(R*вӮҠ)} Оұ*`. Then:*

<!-- label: III.XXI.3.5.2 -->

*(ii) For every `Оұ вҲҲ R`, one has `ord_{рқ’®(RвӮҠ)}(Оұ) = (ПҒ*_{RвӮҠ}, Оұ)`.*[^N.D.E-XXI-12]

**Remark 3.5.3.** *If `w вҲҲ W`, one has `ПҒ_{w(RвӮҠ)} = w(ПҒ_{RвӮҠ})` and `ПҒ*_{w(RвӮҠ)} = w(ПҒ*_{RвӮҠ})`.*

<!-- label: III.XXI.3.5.3 -->

**Proposition 3.5.4.** *Let `Оұ` and `Оі` be two non-proportional roots, with `Оұ` indivisible. There exists a system of
simple roots containing `Оұ` and a root `ОІ` such that `Оі = a Оұ + b ОІ`, with `a, b вҲҲ в„•`.*

<!-- label: III.XXI.3.5.4 -->

*Proof.* Indeed, let us construct a basis of the vector space `V(R)` containing `ОұвӮҒ = Оұ`, `ОұвӮӮ = Оі`. Consider the
lexicographic order with respect to this basis. Denoting by `RвӮҠ` the set of roots `> 0`, it is clear that `RвӮҠ` is a
system of positive roots and[^N.D.E-XXI-13] that the smallest element of `RвӮҠ` not proportional to `Оұ` is simple. This
element is of the form

```text
ОІ = p Оұ + q Оі,    0 < q в©Ҫ 1.
```

One thus has `Оі = qвҒ»В№ ОІ вҲ’ qвҒ»В№ p Оұ`, and since `q > 0`, one has `qвҒ»В№ вҲҲ в„•*` and `вҲ’qвҒ»В№ p вҲҲ в„•`.

Let us finally make two remarks about the group `О“вӮҖ(R)`.

**Proposition 3.5.5.** *Let `G` be an abelian group and `f : R вҶ’ G` a map satisfying the following two conditions:*

<!-- label: III.XXI.3.5.5 -->

*(i) If `Оұ вҲҲ R`, `f(вҲ’Оұ) = вҲ’f(Оұ)`.*

*(ii) If `Оұ, ОІ, Оұ + ОІ вҲҲ R`, `f(Оұ + ОІ) = f(Оұ) + f(ОІ)`.*

*Then there exists a unique group homomorphism `fМ„ : О“вӮҖ(R) вҶ’ G` such that `fМ„(Оұ) = f(Оұ)` for `Оұ вҲҲ R`.*

*Proof.* Indeed, if `О”` is a system of simple roots of `R`, and if `ОІ вҲҲ R` is written `ОЈ_{Оұ вҲҲ О”} a(Оұ) Оұ`, it follows at
once from 3.2.16 that `f(ОІ) = ОЈ_{Оұ вҲҲ О”} a(Оұ) f(Оұ)`. Now `О”` is a basis of `О“вӮҖ(R)`.

<!-- original page 114 -->

**Proposition 3.5.6.** *Let `О”` be a system of simple roots. There exists on `О“вӮҖ(R)` a structure of totally ordered
group such that the roots `> 0` are the elements of `P(О”)` and `Оұ вҶҰ ord_О”(Оұ)` is an increasing function.*

<!-- label: III.XXI.3.5.6 -->

*Proof.* Indeed, let `ОұвӮҒ, ОұвӮӮ, вҖҰ, Оұ_n` (`n = rgss(в„ӣ)`) be the elements of `О”`. For `x вҲҲ О“вӮҖ(R)`, one has a decomposition

```text
x = ОЈбөў mбөў(x) Оұбөў.
```

It suffices to take the lexicographic order with respect to the functions `ОЈ mбөў`, `m_n`, `m_{nвҲ’1}`, вҖҰ, `mвӮӮ`.

**Remark 3.5.7.** *The first roots are in order:*

<!-- label: III.XXI.3.5.7 -->

```text
ОұвӮҒ, ОұвӮӮ, вҖҰ, Оұ_n;
```

*one then has (if these are roots) `2 ОұвӮҒ, ОұвӮҒ + ОұвӮӮ, ОұвӮҒ + ОұвӮғ, вҖҰ`.*

### 3.6. Weyl chambers

<!-- label: III.XXI.3.6 -->

**Lemma 3.6.1.** *Let `V` be a finite-dimensional `в„қ`-vector space.[^N.D.E-XXI-14] Let `fбөў` be independent linear
forms. Set*

<!-- label: III.XXI.3.6.1 -->

```text
C = {x вҲҲ V | fбөў(x) > 0}.
```

*Then `C` is a maximal convex subset of `X = V вҲ’ вӢғбөў fбөўвҒ»В№(0)`.*

*Proof.* Trivial.

<!-- original page 115 -->

**Definition 3.6.2.** *A subset `C` of `V` describable by the procedure of 3.6.1 will be called (here) a* chamber *of
`V`.*

<!-- label: III.XXI.3.6.2 -->

**Definition 3.6.3.** *One says that the hyperplane `H` of `V` is a* wall *of `C` if `H вҲ© (CМ„ вҲ’ C)` contains a nonempty
open subset of `H`.*

<!-- label: III.XXI.3.6.3 -->

**Remark 3.6.4.** *For a convex subset, the closure is described without appeal to the topology of `V`: it is the set
of endpoints of all open segments contained in the given subset.*

<!-- label: III.XXI.3.6.4 -->

**Lemma 3.6.5.** *Under the conditions of 3.6.1, one has*

<!-- label: III.XXI.3.6.5 -->

```text
CМ„ = {x вҲҲ V | fбөў(x) в©ҫ 0}.
```

*The walls of `C` are the hyperplanes `fбөўвҒ»В№(0)`.*

*Proof.* The first assertion is clear. The second then follows from the fact that `CМ„ вҲ’ C вҠӮ вӢғбөў fбөўвҒ»В№(0)` and that the
`fбөўвҒ»В№(0)` are obviously walls of `C`.

**Proposition 3.6.6.** *Let `C` be a chamber of `V`. If `Hбөў`, `i = 1, 2, вҖҰ, n`, are the distinct walls of `C`, then for
every system of linear forms `{uбөў}` such that `Hбөў = uбөўвҒ»В№(0)`, there exist `Оөбөў вҲҲ {вҲ’1, +1}` such that `C` is defined by*

<!-- label: III.XXI.3.6.6 -->

```text
C = {x вҲҲ V | Оөбөў uбөў(x) > 0}.
```

*For every wall `H` of `C`, one has `H вҲ© C = вҲ…` and `H вҲ© CМ„ = C_H`, where `C_H` is a chamber in `H`. The walls of
`C_{H_i}` are the `Hвұј вҲ© Hбөў`, for `j вү  i`.*

*Proof.* This follows trivially from the lemma.

**Definition 3.6.7.** *The `C_{H_i}` are the* faces *of `C`.*

<!-- label: III.XXI.3.6.7 -->

Let now `в„ӣ = (M, M*, R, R*)` be a root datum. We put `V*_в„қ = M* вҠ— в„қ`.

**Definition 3.6.8.**[^N.D.E-XXI-15] *For every `Оұ вҲҲ R`, set*

<!-- label: III.XXI.3.6.8 -->

```text
H_Оұ = {x вҲҲ V*_в„қ | (x, Оұ) = 0}.
```

<!-- original page 116 -->

*We denote `X = V*_в„қ вҲ’ вӢғ_{Оұ вҲҲ R} H_Оұ`. For every `x вҲҲ X`, set*

```text
RвӮҠ(x) = {Оұ вҲҲ R | (x, Оұ) > 0}.
```

*For every system of positive roots `RвӮҠ`, denote*

```text
рқ’һ(RвӮҠ) = {x вҲҲ V*_в„қ | (x, Оұ) > 0 for all Оұ вҲҲ RвӮҠ}.
```

**Proposition 3.6.9.** *(i) For every `x вҲҲ X`, `RвӮҠ(x)` is a system of positive roots. For every system of positive
roots `RвӮҠ`, `рқ’һ(RвӮҠ)` is a chamber in `V*_в„қ`. The `рқ’һ(RвӮҠ)` are the maximal convex subsets of `X`.*

<!-- label: III.XXI.3.6.9 -->

*(ii) Let `О”` be a system of simple roots. One has*

```text
рқ’һ(P(О”)) = {x вҲҲ V*_в„қ | (x, Оұ) > 0    for all Оұ вҲҲ О”}.
```

*The walls of `рқ’һ(P(О”))` are the hyperplanes `H_Оұ = ОұвҒ»В№(0)`, for `Оұ вҲҲ О”`; its faces are the*

```text
C_Оұ = {x вҲҲ V*_в„қ | (x, Оұ) = 0, (x, ОІ) > 0 for ОІ вҲҲ О”, ОІ вү  Оұ}.
```

*(iii) One has the equivalence*

```text
RвӮҠ(x) = RвӮҠ  вҹә  x вҲҲ рқ’һ(RвӮҠ).
```

*Proof.* It is first clear that `RвӮҠ(x)` is a system of positive roots. Since `x вҲҲ рқ’һ(RвӮҠ(x))`, the union of the
`рқ’һ(RвӮҠ(x))` is `X`. Property (iii) is immediate; it follows that the `рқ’һ(RвӮҠ)` form a partition of `X`. The first
assertion of (ii) is evident. It follows at once that `рқ’һ(RвӮҠ)` is a chamber in `V`, which proves the rest of (i).
Putting `C = рқ’һ(P(О”))`, it remains only to remark that

```text
CМ„ вҲ© (V*_в„қ вҲ’ вӢғ_{Оұ вҲҲ R} ОұвҒ»В№(0)) = CМ„ вҲ© (V*_в„қ вҲ’ вӢғ_{Оұ вҲҲ О”} ОұвҒ»В№(0))
```

to complete the proof of (ii) by 3.6.1.

**Definition 3.6.10.** *The `рқ’һ(RвӮҠ)` are called the* Weyl chambers *of the root datum. On the other hand, for every
Weyl chamber `C`, one sets `RвӮҠ(C) = RвӮҠ(x)` for any `x вҲҲ C`.*

<!-- label: III.XXI.3.6.10 -->

<!-- original page 117 -->

**Corollary 3.6.11.** *The maps `RвӮҠ вҶҰ рқ’һ(RвӮҠ)` and `C вҶҰ RвӮҠ(C)` realize a bijective correspondence between systems of
positive roots and Weyl chambers.*

<!-- label: III.XXI.3.6.11 -->

This correspondence is invariant under the Weyl group:

**Lemma 3.6.12.** *If `w вҲҲ W(в„ӣ)`, one has `рқ’һ(w(RвӮҠ)) = w(рқ’һ(RвӮҠ))`.*

<!-- label: III.XXI.3.6.12 -->

**Corollary 3.6.13.** *The correspondences `О” вҶ” RвӮҠ вҶ” C` are isomorphisms of homogeneous spaces under `W(в„ӣ)`.*

<!-- label: III.XXI.3.6.13 -->

We shall see later that these homogeneous spaces are principal (5.5).

**Remark 3.6.14.** *If `C` is a Weyl chamber, then `вҲ’C` is also one, called the* opposite *of `C`. There exists
therefore a `wвӮҖ вҲҲ W` (and in fact a unique one, cf. 5.5) such that `wвӮҖ(C) = вҲ’C`; one calls it the* symmetry *of the
root datum with respect to the Weyl chamber `C` (or with respect to `RвӮҠ(C)` or `рқ’®(RвӮҠ(C))`вҖҰ).*

*(ii) `uвӮҖ = 0`, `uвӮҒ = Оұ`, `u_{2nвҲ’1} = ОІ`, `u_{2n} = 0`.*

*(iii) If `1 < k < 2n вҲ’ 1`, one has `u_k = a_k Оұ + b_k ОІ`, with `a_k, b_k вҲҲ в„•*`.*

*Proof.* Assertion (i) follows from `(s_Оұ s_ОІ)вҒҝ = 1` and `u_{2n} = 0`.

Let us prove (ii) and (iii). Direct computation gives, in the four cases, the following sequences of

```text
(AвӮҒ Г— AвӮҒ)    0, Оұ, ОІ + Оұ, ОІ, 0.

(AвӮӮ)         0, Оұ, ОІ + 2Оұ, 2ОІ + 2Оұ, 2ОІ + Оұ, ОІ, 0.

(BвӮӮ)         0, Оұ, ОІ + 3Оұ, 2ОІ + 4Оұ, 3ОІ + 4Оұ, 3ОІ + 3Оұ, 2ОІ + Оұ, ОІ, 0.

                                                                  4ОІ + 4Оұ, 2ОІ + Оұ, ОІ, 0.
```

**Lemma 4.2.** *Set `w_{2p} = (s_ОІ s_Оұ)^p`, `w_{2p+1} = s_Оұ (s_ОІ s_Оұ)^p`, so that `w_0, вҖҰ, w_{2nвҲ’1}` are the distinct
elements of `W`. Let `u_0, вҖҰ, u_{2nвҲ’1}` be the elements of `V` defined in 4.1. For every `x вҲҲ V*`, one has*

<!-- label: III.XXI.4.2 -->

<!-- original page 120 -->

```text
x вҲ’ w_k(x) = n_k Оұ* + m_k ОІ*,
```

*with `n_k, m_k вҲҲ в„ҡ` and `n_k + m_k = (x, u_k)`.*[^N.D.E-XXI-19]

*Proof.* The proof is by induction on `k`. If `k = 0`, the formula is trivially verified. Let us carry out, for
example, the passage from `w_{2p}` to `w_{2p+1}`. One has `w_{2p+1} = s_Оұ w_{2p}`, whence

```text
x вҲ’ w_{2p+1}(x) = x вҲ’ w_{2p}(x) + w_{2p}(x) вҲ’ s_Оұ w_{2p}(x) = n_{2p} Оұ* + m_{2p} ОІ* + (w_{2p}(x), Оұ) Оұ*.
```

So

```text
n_{2p+1} + m_{2p+1} = n_{2p} + m_{2p} + ((s_ОІ s_Оұ)^p(x), Оұ)
                    = (x, u_{2p}) + (x, (s_Оұ s_ОІ)^p(Оұ)) = (x, u_{2p+1}).
```

**Corollary 4.3.**[^N.D.E-XXI-20] *Let `x вҲҲ V*`. For every `w вҲҲ W`, set*

<!-- label: III.XXI.4.3 -->

```text
x вҲ’ w(x) = a_w Оұ* + b_w ОІ*.
```

*If `(x, Оұ) в©ҫ 0` and `(x, ОІ) в©ҫ 0`, then `a_w + b_w в©ҫ 0`. If moreover `(x, Оұ) > 0` (resp. `(x, ОІ) > 0`), then
`a_w + b_w > 0` for `w вү  1, s_ОІ` (resp. for `w вү  1, s_Оұ`).*

*Proof.* This follows at once from 4.1 and 4.2.

**Corollary 4.4.** *Let `в„ӣ` be any root datum and `О”` a system of simple roots. Let `Оі` be a positive root and `Оұ`, `ОІ`
two simple roots; let `W_{Оұ,ОІ}` be the subgroup of `W` generated by `s_Оұ` and `s_ОІ`. If*

<!-- label: III.XXI.4.4 -->

```text

```

*then, for every `w вҲҲ W_{Оұ,ОІ}`, one has `ord_О”(w(Оі)) в©Ҫ ord_О”(Оі)`; moreover `ord_О”(w(Оі)) < ord_О”(Оі)` if `w вү  1`,
`w вү  s_ОІ`.*

*Proof.* Indeed, consider the dual root datum `в„ӣ*`, then the datum `(M*, M, R'*, R')`, where `R'` is the set of roots

## 5. The Weyl group: generators and relations

<!-- label: III.XXI.5 -->

Let `в„ӣ` be a root datum. Since the Weyl group is the same for this datum and for the corresponding reduced datum, one
may assume `в„ӣ` reduced to study the Weyl group.

Let `О” = {ОұвӮҒ, вҖҰ, Оұ_n}` be a system of simple roots (`n = rgss(в„ӣ)`). Let `n_{ij}` be the order of the element
`s_{Оұ_i} s_{Оұ_j}` of `W`. In particular, one has `n_{ii} = 1`, and we saw in 3.4.2 and 3.4.3 that the subgroup `W_{ij}`
of `W` generated by `s_{Оұ_i}` and `s_{Оұ_j}` was defined by the relations:

```text
s_{Оұ_i}ВІ = s_{Оұ_j}ВІ = (s_{Оұ_i} s_{Оұ_j})^{n_{ij}} = 1.
```

**Theorem 5.1.** *The group `W` is the group generated by the elements `s_{Оұ_i}`, `i = 1, 2, вҖҰ, n`, subject to the
relations `(s_{Оұ_i} s_{Оұ_j})^{n_{ij}} = 1`.*

<!-- label: III.XXI.5.1 -->

*Proof.* We have already seen that the theorem is true when `n = 2`; we shall use this remark in the course of the
proof. Introduce the group `WМғ` generated by elements `T_i`, `i = 1, 2, вҖҰ, n`, subject to the relations
`(T_i T_j)^{n_{ij}} = 1`. One has in particular `n_{ii} = 1`, whence `T_iВІ = 1`. Let `p : WМғ вҶ’ W` be the group morphism
that sends `T_i` to `s_{Оұ_i}`. One knows that `p` is surjective; we shall show it is injective.

<!-- original page 122 -->

**Lemma 5.2.** *One can define in a unique manner for each `Оұ вҲҲ P(О”)` an element `T_Оұ вҲҲ WМғ` such that one has the
following properties:*

<!-- label: III.XXI.5.2 -->

*(i) `p(T_Оұ) = s_Оұ`,*

*(ii) `T_{Оұ_i} = T_i`,*

*(iii) if `ОІ` and `Оұ` are two positive roots such that `s_{Оұ_i}(Оұ) = ОІ`, then `T_i T_Оұ T_i = T_ОІ`.*

<!-- original page 87 -->

*Proof.* Remark first that it follows from 1.2.10 and 3.3.6 that (i) is a consequence of (ii) and (iii), and that (ii)
and (iii) completely determine the `T_Оұ`. We shall make the construction by induction on `ord_О”(Оұ)`. If
`ord_О”(Оұ) = 1`, then `Оұ вҲҲ О”` and one sets `T_Оұ = T_i` if `Оұ = Оұ_i`. Consider the hypothesis:

(H_p) *there exist `T_Оұ`, for `Оұ вҲҲ P(О”)`, `ord_О”(Оұ) в©Ҫ p`, satisfying (ii) and condition (iii) whenever
`ord_О”(Оұ) в©Ҫ p`, `ord_О”(ОІ) в©Ҫ p`.*

This is verified for `p = 1`: indeed, if `Оұ` and `s_{Оұ_i}(Оұ) = ОІ` are simple, `Оұ_i` and `Оұ` are orthogonal, so if one
denotes `Оұ_j = Оұ = ОІ`, one has `n_{ij} = 2`, whence

```text
T_i T_j T_i = T_j.
```

Suppose `p > 1` and `(H_{pвҲ’1})` verified.

*A) Construction of the `T_Оұ` for `ord_О”(Оұ) в©Ҫ p`.* It evidently suffices to do this for `ord_О”(Оұ) = p`. There then
exists `Оұ_i вҲҲ О”` such that `s_{Оұ_i}(Оұ) вҲҲ P(О”)` and `ord_О”(s_{Оұ_i}(Оұ)) < p` (3.3.3). One then sets

<!-- original page 123 -->

```text
(вӢҶ)    T_Оұ = T_i T_{s_{Оұ_i}(Оұ)} T_i.
```

Let us verify that `T_Оұ` depends only on `Оұ`. Let `Оұ_j вҲҲ О”` be such that `s_{Оұ_j}(Оұ) вҲҲ P(О”)` and `ord_О”(s_{Оұ_j}(Оұ)) < p`.
Let us prove that

```text
(+)    T_i T_{s_{Оұ_i}(Оұ)} T_i = T_j T_{s_{Оұ_j}(Оұ)} T_j.
```

Let us distinguish two cases.

*(1)* Suppose `Оұ` is a linear combination of `Оұ_i` and `Оұ_j`. Then the same is true of `s_{Оұ_i}(Оұ)` and `s_{Оұ_j}(Оұ)`,
and by `(H_{pвҲ’1})`, `T_{s_{Оұ_i}(Оұ)}` and `T_{s_{Оұ_j}(Оұ)}` are written as words in `T_i` and `T_j`. Since the projection
of (+) in `W` is verified, and since the theorem is true for `n = 2`, so `p` is injective on the subgroup of `WМғ`
generated by `T_i` and `T_j`, (+) is indeed verified.

*(2)* Suppose `Оұ` is not a linear combination of `Оұ_i` and `Оұ_j`. Then if `w вҲҲ W_{Оұ_i, Оұ_j}`, the `w(Оұ)` will all be
positive (cf. 3.4.9). The relation to be verified is also written

```text
(++)    (T_i T_j)^{n_{ij} вҲ’ 1} T_{s_{Оұ_i}(Оұ)} (T_j T_i)^{n_{ij} вҲ’ 1} = T_{s_{Оұ_j}(Оұ)}.
```

Now it follows from 4.4 that the `w(Оұ)` are all of order `< p` for `w вҲҲ W_{Оұ_i, Оұ_j}`, `w вү  1`. One can therefore apply
hypothesis `(H_{pвҲ’1})` `2(n_{ij} вҲ’ 1)` times, and we are done.

*B) Verification of `(H_p)`.*[^N.D.E-XXI-22] One must verify that if `Оұ_j вҲҲ О”` and if `ОІ = s_{Оұ_j}(Оұ)` satisfies
`ord_О”(ОІ) в©Ҫ p`, then `T_j T_Оұ T_j = T_ОІ`. If `ord_О”(ОІ) < p`, this follows from what precedes (since `T_jВІ = 1`), so one
may suppose `ord_О”(ОІ) = p = ord_О”(Оұ)`. In this case, `Оұ` and `Оұ_j` are orthogonal, so `ОІ = Оұ`, and it is a matter of
seeing that one has

<!-- original page 124 -->

```text
(вҖ )    T_j T_Оұ T_j = T_Оұ.
```

By `(вӢҶ)` above, one has `T_Оұ = T_i T_{s_{Оұ_i}(Оұ)} T_i`, so it remains only to verify the following equality:

```text
(+++)    T_j T_i T_{s_{Оұ_i}(Оұ)} T_i T_j = T_Оұ = T_i T_{s_{Оұ_i}(Оұ)} T_i.
```

Write `m = n_{ij}` and `s_i = s_{Оұ_i}`, `s_j = s_{Оұ_j}`. One has `T_j T_i = (T_i T_j)^{mвҲ’1}` and, by 4.4, one has
`ord_О”(w(Оұ)) < p` for every `w вҲҲ W_{ij}` distinct from `1 = (s_i s_j)^m` and from `s_j = s_i (s_i s_j)^{mвҲ’1}`. One
deduces, by the induction hypothesis, that

```text
T_j (T_i T_j)^{mвҲ’2} T_{s_i(Оұ)} (T_j T_i)^{mвҲ’2} T_j = T_{s_j (s_i s_j)^{mвҲ’2} s_i(Оұ)} = T_{s_i s_j(Оұ)} = T_{s_i(Оұ)}
```

(the last equality since `s_j(Оұ) = Оұ`), whence finally

```text
(T_i T_j)^{mвҲ’1} T_{s_i(Оұ)} (T_j T_i)^{mвҲ’1} = T_i T_{s_i(Оұ)} T_i
```

which proves (+++).

**Lemma 5.3.** *Let `h вҲҲ WМғ`. Write it*

<!-- label: III.XXI.5.3 -->

```text
h = T_{Оұ_1} вӢҜ T_{Оұ_m}
```

*with the `Оұ_i вҲҲ О”`, not necessarily distinct, in such a way that `m` is minimal. Then*

```text
p(h)(Оұ_m) вҲҲ вҲ’P(О”).
```

*Proof.* Indeed, since `p(T_{Оұ_m})(Оұ_m) = s_{Оұ_m}(Оұ_m) = вҲ’Оұ_m`, if `p(h)(Оұ_m)` were positive, there would exist an index
`k`, `1 в©Ҫ k в©Ҫ m вҲ’ 1`, such that

```text
u = s_{Оұ_{k+1}} вӢҜ s_{Оұ_m}(Оұ_m) = вҲ’s_{Оұ_{k+1}} вӢҜ s_{Оұ_{mвҲ’1}}(Оұ_m) вҲҲ вҲ’P(О”),
```

and `s_{Оұ_k}(u) вҲҲ P(О”)`. But then one has necessarily `u = вҲ’Оұ_k` (3.3.1), whence

```text
s_{Оұ_{k+1}} вӢҜ s_{Оұ_{mвҲ’1}}(Оұ_m) = Оұ_k,
```

which entails by (iii)

```text
T_{Оұ_k} T_{Оұ_{k+1}} вӢҜ T_{Оұ_{mвҲ’1}} T_{Оұ_m} = T_{Оұ_{k+1}} вӢҜ T_{Оұ_{mвҲ’1}},
```

and this contradicts the minimal character of `m`.

<!-- original page 125 -->

Let now `h вҲҲ WМғ` be such that `p(h)(P(О”)) вҠӮ P(О”)`. By Lemma 5.3, one has `p(h) = 1`, which proves Theorem 5.1 and
moreover the

**Corollary 5.4.** *If `RвӮҠ` is a system of positive roots and if `w вҲҲ W` is such that `w(RвӮҠ) = RвӮҠ`, then `w = 1`.*

<!-- label: III.XXI.5.4 -->

**Corollary 5.5.** *The Weyl group operates freely and transitively on the set of systems of positive roots (resp. of
systems of simple roots, resp. of Weyl chambers).*

<!-- label: III.XXI.5.5 -->

Let us now choose a system of simple roots `О”`. Put `RвҒә = P(О”)`.[^N.D.E-XXI-23]

For every pair of simple roots `(Оұ, ОІ) вҲҲ О” Г— О”`, denote by `R_{Оұ,ОІ}` the set of roots that are linear combinations of
`Оұ` and `ОІ`. Write `RвҒә_{Оұ,ОІ} = RвҒә вҲ© R_{Оұ,ОІ}` and let `W_{Оұ,ОІ}` be the Weyl group of `R_{Оұ,ОІ}`, that is to say, the
subgroup of `W` generated by `s_Оұ` and `s_ОІ`.

**Theorem 5.6 (Tits).** *Let `Оұ` and `ОІ` be two simple roots and let `w вҲҲ W` be such that `w(Оұ) = ОІ`. There exists a
sequence of simple roots `ОұвӮҖ, вҖҰ, Оұ_m` and a sequence `wвӮҖ, вҖҰ, w_{mвҲ’1}` of elements of `W` satisfying the following
conditions:*

<!-- label: III.XXI.5.6 -->

<!-- original page 89 -->

*(i) `ОұвӮҖ = Оұ`, `Оұ_m = ОІ`.*

*(ii) `w = w_{mвҲ’1} w_{mвҲ’2} вӢҜ w_0`.*

*(iii) `w_i(Оұ_i) = Оұ_{i+1}`, for `0 в©Ҫ i в©Ҫ m вҲ’ 1`.*

*(iv) For every `i`, `0 в©Ҫ i в©Ҫ m вҲ’ 1`, such that `Оұ_i вү  Оұ_{i+1}`, one has `w_i вҲҲ W_{Оұ_i, Оұ_{i+1}}`.*

*(v) For every `i`, `0 в©Ҫ i в©Ҫ m вҲ’ 1`, such that `Оұ_i = Оұ_{i+1}`, there exists a simple root `ОІ_i` such that
`w_i вҲҲ W_{Оұ_i, ОІ_i}`.*

*Proof.* Set[^N.D.E-XXI-24]

<!-- original page 126 -->

```text
M(w) = Card(RвҒә вҲ© wвҒ»В№(вҲ’RвҒә)) = Card({Оұ вҲҲ RвҒә | w(Оұ) вҲҲ вҲ’RвҒә}).
```

If `M(w) = 0`, then `w(RвҒә) = RвҒә`, so `w = 1` by 5.4 and the theorem is trivial (`m = 0`; assertions (iii) to (v) are
empty). We argue by induction on `M(w)`. If `M(w) > 0`, there exists `ОІ_0 вҲҲ О”` such that `w(ОІ_0) вҲҲ вҲ’RвҒә`. Set `Оұ_0 = Оұ`.
Consider the set

```text
A = wвҒ»В№(RвҒә) вҲ© R_{Оұ_0, ОІ_0}.
```

This is a system of positive roots of `R_{Оұ_0, ОІ_0}`. There thus exists `w_0 вҲҲ W_{Оұ_0, ОІ_0}` such that

```text
w_0вҒ»В№(RвҒә_{Оұ_0, ОІ_0}) = A.
```

Put `w' = w w_0вҒ»В№`. By 3.4.9, one has at once

```text
RвҒә вҲ’ RвҒә_{Оұ_0, ОІ_0} = w_0(RвҒә вҲ’ RвҒә_{Оұ_0, ОІ_0}),
```

whence

```text
(1)    (RвҒә вҲ’ RвҒә_{Оұ_0, ОІ_0}) вҲ© wвҒ»В№(вҲ’RвҒә) = w_0((RвҒә вҲ’ RвҒә_{Оұ_0, ОІ_0}) вҲ© w'вҒ»В№(вҲ’RвҒә)).
```

On the other hand,

```text
(2)    ОІ_0 вҲҲ RвҒә_{Оұ_0, ОІ_0} вҲ© wвҒ»В№(вҲ’RвҒә),
```

and, since `w_0(R_{Оұ_0, ОІ_0}) = R_{Оұ_0, ОІ_0}`, one has `w_0(вҲ’A) = R_{Оұ_0, ОІ_0} вҲ© w'вҒ»В№(вҲ’RвҒә)`, whence

```text
(2вҖІ)    RвҒә_{Оұ_0, ОІ_0} вҲ© w'вҒ»В№(вҲ’RвҒә) = RвҒә_{Оұ_0, ОІ_0} вҲ© w_0(вҲ’A) = RвҒә_{Оұ_0, ОІ_0} вҲ© вҲ’RвҒә_{Оұ_0, ОІ_0} = вҲ….
```

It follows from (1), (2), (2вҖІ) that `M(w') < M(w)`.

Set `Оұ_1 = w_0(Оұ_0)`; let us show that `Оұ_1 вҲҲ О”`, that is to say `Оұ_0 вҲҲ w_0вҒ»В№(О”)`. One knows that `w(Оұ_0) вҲҲ О”`, so
`Оұ_0 вҲҲ wвҒ»В№(О”)`, hence also `Оұ_0 вҲҲ wвҒ»В№(О”) вҲ© R_{Оұ_0, ОІ_0}`; so `Оұ_0` is a simple root of
`A = wвҒ»В№(RвҒә) вҲ© R_{Оұ_0, ОІ_0} = w_0вҒ»В№(RвҒә_{Оұ_0, ОІ_0})`, hence belongs to

```text
w_0вҒ»В№(О” вҲ© RвҒә_{Оұ_0, ОІ_0}) = w_0вҒ»В№({Оұ_0, ОІ_0})
```

(see 3.4.8). So `Оұ_1 = w_0(Оұ_0)` equals `Оұ_0` or `ОІ_0`. If `Оұ_1 вү  Оұ_0`, one has `Оұ_1 = ОІ_0` and `w_0 вҲҲ W_{Оұ_0, Оұ_1}`.

Finally, one has `ОІ = w'(Оұ_1)`, with `M(w') < M(w)`, and one concludes by induction.

## 6. Morphisms of root data

<!-- label: III.XXI.6 -->

<!-- original page 127 -->

### 6.1. Definition

<!-- label: III.XXI.6.1 -->

Let `в„ӣ = (M, M*, R, R*)` and `в„ӣ' = (M', M'*, R', R'*)` be two root data. Let `f : M' вҶ’ M` be a linear map and
`бө—f : M* вҶ’ M'*` the transposed map.

**Definition 6.1.1.** *One says that `f` is a* morphism *from `в„ӣ'` to `в„ӣ` and writes `f : в„ӣ' вҶ’ в„ӣ`, if `f` induces a
bijection of `R'` onto `R` and `бө—f` a bijection of `R*` onto `R'*`.*

<!-- label: III.XXI.6.1.1 -->

Then `бө—f` is a morphism of the dual root data:

```text
бө—f : в„ӣ* вҹ¶ в„ӣ'*.
```

One sees easily that if `f` is a morphism from `в„ӣ'` to `в„ӣ`, and if one writes `Оұ = f(Оұ')` for `Оұ' вҲҲ R'`, one has
`Оұ'* = бө—f(Оұ*)`. Indeed, one sees immediately that if one writes `p` and `p'` for the maps of 1.2.1 respective to `в„ӣ`
and `в„ӣ'`, one has `p' = бө—f вҲҳ p вҲҳ f`, and the assertion sought follows at once. We leave to the reader the task of
proving the statements that follow, which are almost all trivial.

**Proposition 6.1.2.** *Let `f : в„ӣ' вҶ’ в„ӣ` be a morphism of root data. If `Оұ' вҲҲ R'` and `Оұ = f(Оұ')`, then
`Оұ'* = бө—f(Оұ*)`. Moreover, `f` induces isomorphisms:*

<!-- label: III.XXI.6.1.2 -->

```text
R' вҘІ R,    О“вӮҖ(R') вҘІ О“вӮҖ(R),    V(R') вҘІ V(R),
```

*and `бө—f` induces isomorphisms:*

```text
R* вҘІ R'*,    О“вӮҖ(R*) вҘІ О“вӮҖ(R'*),    V(R*) вҘІ V(R'*),
```

<!-- original page 128 -->

*the last being the transpose of the corresponding morphism induced by `f`. The map `s_{Оұ'} вҶҰ s_{f(Оұ')}` extends to an
isomorphism `W(в„ӣ') вҘІ W(в„ӣ)` compatible with the operations of these two groups on the sets of 1.1.13.*

**Proposition 6.1.3.** *The maps*

<!-- label: III.XXI.6.1.3 -->

```text
О”' вҶҰ f(О”'),    R'вӮҠ вҶҰ f(R'вӮҠ),    C' вҶҰ (бө—f вҠ— в„қ)вҒ»В№(C')
```

*define bijective correspondences between systems of simple roots, systems of positive roots, and Weyl chambers for
`в„ӣ'` and `в„ӣ`. These correspondences are compatible with the action of the Weyl groups and with the correspondences*

```text
рқ’®(RвӮҠ) вҶ” RвӮҠ вҶ” рқ’һ(RвӮҠ).
```

**Lemma 6.1.4.** *Morphisms compose. For the morphism `f : в„ӣ' вҶ’ в„ӣ` to be an isomorphism, it is necessary and sufficient
that `f : M' вҶ’ M` be bijective.*

<!-- label: III.XXI.6.1.4 -->

### 6.2. Isogenies

<!-- label: III.XXI.6.2 -->

**Definition 6.2.1.** *A morphism `f : в„ӣ' вҶ’ в„ӣ` of root data is called an* isogeny *if `f : M' вҶ’ M` is injective with
finite cokernel.*

<!-- label: III.XXI.6.2.1 -->

*If `f` is an isogeny, then `бө—f` is an isogeny.*

**Definition 6.2.2.** *Let `f : в„ӣ' вҶ’ в„ӣ` be an isogeny. Set `K(f) = Coker(M' вҶ’^{f} M)`.*

<!-- label: III.XXI.6.2.2 -->

**Lemma 6.2.3.** *One has a natural pairing*

<!-- label: III.XXI.6.2.3 -->

```text
K(f) Г— K(бө—f) вҶ’ в„ҡ/в„Ө,
```

*which puts these two finite groups in duality.*

*Proof.* This is classical.

**Lemma 6.2.4.** *If `f : в„ӣ' вҶ’ в„ӣ` is a morphism, then `rgss(в„ӣ') = rgss(в„ӣ)`. If moreover `f` is an isogeny, one has also
`rgred(в„ӣ') = rgred(в„ӣ)`.*

<!-- label: III.XXI.6.2.4 -->

<!-- original page 129 -->

*Proof.* Trivial.

**Lemma 6.2.5.** *Every morphism of semisimple root data is an isogeny.*

<!-- label: III.XXI.6.2.5 -->

*Proof.* This follows at once from the fact that `f` must induce an isomorphism of `V' = V(R')` onto `V = V(R)`.

If `в„ӣ'` and `в„ӣ` are semisimple, every isogeny `f : в„ӣ' вҶ’ в„ӣ` defines a commutative diagram:

```text
О“вӮҖ(R') вҘІ О“вӮҖ(R)
  вҠӮ        вҠӮ
  вҶ“        вҶ“
  M'   вҶ’   M.
       f
```

If `M = О“вӮҖ(R)`, then `f` is necessarily an isomorphism.

**Definition 6.2.6.** *A root datum is called* adjoint *(resp.* simply connected*) if `M = О“вӮҖ(R)`, resp.
`M* = О“вӮҖ(R*)`.*

<!-- label: III.XXI.6.2.6 -->

An adjoint or simply connected root datum is therefore semisimple. On the other hand, `в„ӣ` is adjoint (resp. simply
connected) if and only if `в„ӣ*` is simply connected (resp. adjoint). By virtue of the preceding result, one has:

**Proposition 6.2.7.** *Let `в„ӣ` be a semisimple root datum. The following conditions are equivalent:*

<!-- label: III.XXI.6.2.7 -->

*(i) `в„ӣ` is adjoint (resp. simply connected).*

*(ii) Every isogeny `в„ӣ' вҶ’ в„ӣ` (resp. `в„ӣ вҶ’ в„ӣ'`) is an isomorphism.*

**Proposition 6.2.8.** *Let `в„ӣ` be an adjoint (resp. simply connected) root datum. Every indivisible root (resp.
coroot) is an indivisible element of `M` (resp. `M*`).*

<!-- label: III.XXI.6.2.8 -->

*Proof.* Indeed, every indivisible root is part of a basis of `О“вӮҖ(R)`, by 3.3.5.

### 6.3. Radical and coradical

<!-- label: III.XXI.6.3 -->

Let `в„ӣ` be a root datum. Set

```text
N = {x вҲҲ M | (Оұ*, x) = 0 for all Оұ* вҲҲ R*};
N* = M*/(V(R*) вҲ© M*).
```

**Lemma 6.3.1.** *Consider the canonical morphisms:*

<!-- label: III.XXI.6.3.1 -->

```text
N вҹ¶ M,    M* вҹ¶ N*.
```

*They are transposes of each other and `N*` is identified with the dual of `N`.*

*Proof.* This is immediate, taking 1.2.5 into account.

**Definition 6.3.2.** *One calls* coradical *of `в„ӣ` and denotes by `corad(в„ӣ)` the trivial root datum*

<!-- label: III.XXI.6.3.2 -->

```text
corad(в„ӣ) = (N, N*, вҲ…, вҲ…).
```

*If one sets `в„ӣ_0 = (M, M*, вҲ…, вҲ…)` (this is a trivial root datum), one has therefore a morphism*

```text
corad(в„ӣ) вҹ¶ в„ӣ_0.
```

**Definition 6.3.3.** *One calls* radical *of `в„ӣ` and denotes by `rad(в„ӣ)` the trivial root datum:*

<!-- label: III.XXI.6.3.3 -->

```text
rad(в„ӣ) = corad(в„ӣ*)*.
```

[^N.D.E-XXI-25]

One thus has a diagram

```text
       corad(в„ӣ) в”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”ҖвҶ’ rad(в„ӣ)
            вҶҳ            вҶҷ
               в„ӣ_0
```

<!-- original page 131 -->

whose transpose is the corresponding diagram for `в„ӣ*`.

**Lemma 6.3.4.** *The canonical morphism `u : corad(в„ӣ) вҶ’ rad(в„ӣ)` is an isogeny.*

<!-- label: III.XXI.6.3.4 -->

**Definition 6.3.5.** *Set `N(в„ӣ) = K(u) = M / ((V(R) вҲ© M) + вӢӮ_{Оұ вҲҲ R} Ker(Оұ*))`. One then has a canonical pairing*

<!-- label: III.XXI.6.3.5 -->

```text
N(в„ӣ) Г— N(в„ӣ*) вҹ¶ в„ҡ/в„Ө.
```

**Lemma 6.3.6.** *One has `rgred(rad(в„ӣ)) = rgred(corad(в„ӣ)) = rgred(в„ӣ) вҲ’ rgss(в„ӣ)`, and the following conditions are
equivalent:*

<!-- label: III.XXI.6.3.6 -->

*(i) `в„ӣ` is semisimple,*

*(ii) `rad(в„ӣ) = 0`,*

*(iii) `corad(в„ӣ) = 0`.*

### 6.4. Products of root data

<!-- label: III.XXI.6.4 -->

**Definition 6.4.1.** *Let `в„ӣ = (M, M*, R, R*)` and `в„ӣ' = (M', M'*, R', R'*)` be two root data. One calls* product root
datum *of `в„ӣ` and `в„ӣ'` and denotes by `в„ӣ'' = в„ӣ Г— в„ӣ'` the root datum `(M'', M''*, R'', R''*)` where*

<!-- label: III.XXI.6.4.1 -->

```text
M'' = M Г— M',                 M''* = M* Г— M'*,
R'' = (R Г— 0) вҲӘ (0 Г— R'),     R''* = (R* Г— 0) вҲӘ (0 Г— R'*),
```

*the map `Оұ вҶҰ Оұ*` being the obvious map.*

**Proposition 6.4.2.** *Under the preceding conditions, one has canonical isomorphisms*

<!-- label: III.XXI.6.4.2 -->

```text
О“вӮҖ(R'') вүғ О“вӮҖ(R) Г— О“вӮҖ(R'),    V(R'') вүғ V(R) Г— V(R'),
W(в„ӣ'') вүғ W(в„ӣ) Г— W(в„ӣ'),
```

*etc., and the equalities*

<!-- original page 132 -->

```text
rgred(в„ӣ'') = rgred(в„ӣ) + rgred(в„ӣ'),    rgss(в„ӣ'') = rgss(в„ӣ) + rgss(в„ӣ').
```

*One also has a canonical isomorphism of root data*

```text
(в„ӣ Г— в„ӣ')* вүғ в„ӣ* Г— в„ӣ'*.
```

The preceding definitions extend at once to a product of several factors. One has at once:

**Proposition 6.4.3.** *Let `в„ӣ = в„ӣ_1 Г— в„ӣ_2 Г— вӢҜ Г— в„ӣ_n` be a product of root data. The following conditions are
equivalent:*

<!-- label: III.XXI.6.4.3 -->

*(i) `в„ӣ` is semisimple (resp. simply connected, resp. adjoint, resp. reduced).*

*(ii) Each `в„ӣ_i` is semisimple (resp. simply connected, resp. adjoint, resp. reduced).*

Let us consider the following special case: let `в„ӣ_0` be a trivial root datum and `в„ӣ_1` a semisimple root datum. One
then has a commutative diagram

```text
       в„ӣ_0 Г— в„ӣ_1
        вҶ—      вҶҳ
      вҶ—          вҶҳ
   в„ӣ_1   в”Җв”Җidв”Җв”ҖвҶ’  в„ӣ_1.
```

**Lemma 6.4.4.** *One has canonical isomorphisms*

<!-- label: III.XXI.6.4.4 -->

```text
corad(в„ӣ_0 Г— в„ӣ_1) вҘІ rad(в„ӣ_0 Г— в„ӣ_1)
        вҶҳ             вҶҷ
        вүғ           вүғ
           в„ӣ_0.
```

*In particular, `N(в„ӣ_0 Г— в„ӣ_1) = 0`.*

<!-- original page 94 -->

We shall see later that if conversely `N(в„ӣ) = 0`, then the root datum `в„ӣ` is a product of a semisimple datum by a
trivial datum.

### 6.5. Induced and coinduced root data

<!-- label: III.XXI.6.5 -->

Let `в„ӣ = (M, M*, R, R*)` be a root datum. Let `N вҠӮ M` be a subgroup containing the roots, i.e. such that

```text
О“вӮҖ(R) вҠӮ N вҠӮ M.
```

The canonical linear map `i_N : N вҶ’ M` gives by transposition a linear map

```text
бө—i_N : M* вҹ¶ N*.
```

Set `R_N = R` and `R*_N = бө—i_N(R*)`.

**Lemma 6.5.1.** *`в„ӣ_N = (N, N*, R_N, R*_N)` is a root datum, and `i_N` a morphism.*

<!-- label: III.XXI.6.5.1 -->

*Proof.* Let us first show that `бө—i_N` induces an isomorphism of `R*` onto `R*_N`. If `Оұ, ОІ вҲҲ R` and
`бө—i_N(Оұ*) = бө—i_N(ОІ*)`, one has `(Оұ*, x) = (ОІ*, x)` for all `x вҲҲ N`, in particular for `x вҲҲ R`, which gives `Оұ = ОІ` by
1.1.4. The rest follows without difficulty.

**Definition 6.5.2.** *`в„ӣ_N` is called the* root datum induced by `в„ӣ` on `N`.

<!-- label: III.XXI.6.5.2 -->

**Lemma 6.5.3.** *Let `f : в„ӣ' вҶ’ в„ӣ` be a morphism. Set `N = f(M') вҠӮ M`. Then `f` factors uniquely through `i_N`.*

<!-- label: III.XXI.6.5.3 -->

In particular, isogenies `в„ӣ' вҶ’ в„ӣ`, up to isomorphism, correspond bijectively to the subgroups of finite index of
`M/О“вӮҖ(R)`, which makes 6.2.7 more precise.

Let now `N*` be a subgroup of `M*` containing `R*`. One defines the root datum *coinduced* by `в„ӣ` on `N*` by

```text
в„ӣ^{N*} = (в„ӣ*_{N*})*,
```

<!-- original page 134 -->

and one has a canonical morphism:

```text
p_{N*} : в„ӣ вҹ¶ в„ӣ^{N*}.
```

**Lemma 6.5.4.** *Let `f : в„ӣ' вҹ¶ в„ӣ` be a morphism. There exist subgroups `N вҠӮ M` and `N'* вҠӮ M'*` such that `f` factors
as*

<!-- label: III.XXI.6.5.4 -->

```text
        f
в„ӣ' в”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”ҖвҶ’ в„ӣ
вҶ“                вҶ‘
p_{N'*}          i_N
вҶ“     f_0        вҶ‘
в„ӣ'^{N'*} в”Җв”ҖвҘІв”Җв”ҖвҶ’ в„ӣ_N,
```

*where `f_0` is an isomorphism.*

*Proof.* Indeed, one takes `N = f(M')` as in 6.5.3. The morphism `M' вҶ’ N` obtained is surjective, so its transpose is
injective. One takes the image of the latter as `N'*`.

Let us now treat certain special cases. If one takes `N = О“вӮҖ(R)`, one writes `в„ӣ_N = ad(в„ӣ)`. If one takes
`N = V(R) вҲ© M`, one writes `в„ӣ_N = ss(в„ӣ)`. One thus has a diagram:

```text
ad(в„ӣ) вҹ¶ ss(в„ӣ) вҹ¶ в„ӣ.
```

Set `dГ©r(в„ӣ) = ss(в„ӣ*)*` and `sc(в„ӣ) = ad(в„ӣ*)*`; by duality, one obtains a diagram:

```text
в„ӣ вҹ¶ dГ©r(в„ӣ) вҹ¶ sc(в„ӣ).
```

**Proposition 6.5.5.** *(i) In the first row of the diagram*

<!-- label: III.XXI.6.5.5 -->

```text
ad(в„ӣ) вҹ¶ ss(в„ӣ) вҹ¶ dГ©r(в„ӣ) вҹ¶ sc(в„ӣ)
            вҶҳ         вҶ—
              в„ӣ
```

*the four data are semisimple and the three morphisms are isogenies.*

*(ii) `ad(в„ӣ)` is an adjoint datum, and `в„ӣ` is adjoint if and only if `ad(в„ӣ) вҶ’ в„ӣ` is an isomorphism.*

<!-- original page 135 -->

*(iii) `sc(в„ӣ)` is a simply connected datum, and `в„ӣ` is simply connected if and only if `в„ӣ вҶ’ sc(в„ӣ)` is an isomorphism.*

*(iv) The following conditions are equivalent:*

*(a) `в„ӣ` is semisimple,*

*(b) `ss(в„ӣ) вҶ’ в„ӣ` is an isomorphism,*

*(c) `в„ӣ вҶ’ dГ©r(в„ӣ)` is an isomorphism.*

Let us pause briefly on the morphism `ss(в„ӣ) вҶ’ dГ©r(в„ӣ)`. Referring to the construction of `ss(в„ӣ)` and `dГ©r(в„ӣ)`, it is
easy to prove the

**Lemma 6.5.6.** *Let `h : ss(в„ӣ) вҶ’ dГ©r(в„ӣ)` be the canonical isogeny. One has `K(h) вүғ N(в„ӣ)`.*

<!-- label: III.XXI.6.5.6 -->

**6.5.7.** Consider other special cases of induced data. Set

<!-- label: III.XXI.6.5.7 -->

```text
N = {x вҲҲ M | (Оұ*, x) = 0 for Оұ вҲҲ R} Г— О“вӮҖ(R);
```

one knows that the sum is direct by 1.2.5. It follows that the root datum `в„ӣ_N` is identified with the product
`ad(в„ӣ) Г— corad(в„ӣ)`.

One can do the same by replacing `О“вӮҖ(R)` by `V(R) вҲ© M`, then dualize these two constructions. One thus obtains a
diagram of root data:

```text
ad(в„ӣ) вҶ’ ss(в„ӣ) вҶ’ dГ©r(в„ӣ) вҶ’ sc(в„ӣ)
  вҶ“       вҶ“        вҶ“        вҶ“
ad(в„ӣ) Г— corad(в„ӣ) вҶ’ ss(в„ӣ) Г— corad(в„ӣ) вҶ’ в„ӣ вҶ’ dГ©r(в„ӣ) Г— rad(в„ӣ) вҶ’ sc(в„ӣ) Г— rad(в„ӣ)
  вҶ“       вҶ“        вҶ“        вҶ“
ad(в„ӣ) вҶ’ ss(в„ӣ) вҶ’ dГ©r(в„ӣ) вҶ’ sc(в„ӣ);
```

<!-- original page 136 -->

which is commutative, as one verifies at once. This diagram is self-dual in an obvious sense. The horizontal morphisms
are isogenies. The composites of the vertical arrows are the identity.

**Lemma 6.5.8.** *Let `h_1` and `h_2` be the canonical isogenies:*

<!-- label: III.XXI.6.5.8 -->

```text
ss(в„ӣ) Г— corad(в„ӣ) в”Җв”Җh_1в”Җв”ҖвҶ’ в„ӣ в”Җв”Җh_2в”Җв”ҖвҶ’ dГ©r(в„ӣ) Г— rad(в„ӣ).
```

*One has `K(h_1) вүғ K(h_2) вүғ N(в„ӣ)`.*

*Proof.* This is trivial from the definitions.

**Corollary 6.5.9.** *Let `в„ӣ` be a root datum. The following conditions are equivalent:*

<!-- label: III.XXI.6.5.9 -->

*(i) `N(в„ӣ) = 0`, i.e. `corad(в„ӣ) вҶ’ rad(в„ӣ)` is an isomorphism.*

*(ii) `h : ss(в„ӣ) вҶ’ dГ©r(в„ӣ)` is an isomorphism.*

*(iii) `h_1 : ss(в„ӣ) Г— corad(в„ӣ) вҶ’ в„ӣ` is an isomorphism.*

*(iv) `h_2 : в„ӣ вҶ’ dГ©r(в„ӣ) Г— rad(в„ӣ)` is an isomorphism.*

*(v) `в„ӣ` is the product of a semisimple datum and a trivial datum.*

Let us also state a trivial consequence of the preceding remarks:

**Corollary 6.5.10.** *For every root datum `в„ӣ`, there exist isogenies*

<!-- label: III.XXI.6.5.10 -->

```text
ad(в„ӣ) Г— в„ӣ_0 вҹ¶ в„ӣ вҹ¶ sc(в„ӣ) Г— в„ӣ_0,
```

*where `в„ӣ_0` is "the" trivial root datum of rank `rgred(в„ӣ) вҲ’ rgss(в„ӣ)`.*

Let us finally note a result that may be useful:

**Lemma 6.5.11.** *Let `в„ӣ` be a root datum, `О”` a system of simple roots, `О”'` a subset of `О”`; consider the root datum
(cf. 3.4.7)*

<!-- label: III.XXI.6.5.11 -->

```text
в„ӣ_{О”'} = (M, M*, R_{О”'}, R*_{О”'}).
```

<!-- original page 137 -->

*(i) If `в„ӣ` is simply connected, then `dГ©r(в„ӣ_{О”'})` is simply connected.*

*(ii) If `в„ӣ` is adjoint, then `ss(в„ӣ_{О”'})` is adjoint.*

*Proof.* The two assertions are evidently equivalent by duality. The second reduces to verifying the formula:

```text
M вҲ© V(R_{О”'}) = О“вӮҖ(R_{О”'});
```

now, if `M = О“вӮҖ(R)`, the two sides are equal to the subgroup of `M` generated by `О”'`.

### 6.6. Weights

<!-- label: III.XXI.6.6 -->

**Definition 6.6.1.** *Let `в„ӣ` be a root datum. Set[^N.D.E-XXI-26]*

<!-- label: III.XXI.6.6.1 -->

```text
Оӣ(в„ӣ) = {x вҲҲ V(R) | (Оұ*, x) вҲҲ в„Ө for all Оұ* вҲҲ R*}.
```

*The elements of `Оӣ(в„ӣ)` are called the* weights *of `в„ӣ`. The weights of `в„ӣ*` are called the* coweights *of `в„ӣ`.*

One has `О“вӮҖ(R) вҠӮ Оӣ(в„ӣ)` and `Оӣ(в„ӣ)` is stable under `W(в„ӣ)`.

**Lemma 6.6.2.** *The bilinear map `V* Г— V вҶ’ в„ҡ` induces a duality*

<!-- label: III.XXI.6.6.2 -->

```text
О“вӮҖ(R*) Г— Оӣ(в„ӣ) вҹ¶ в„Ө.
```

*Proof.* Trivial.

**Corollary 6.6.3.** *Let `О”* = (Оұ*вӮҒ, вҖҰ, Оұ*_n)` be a system of simple coroots. Let `p_i`, `i = 1, 2, вҖҰ, n`, be the
elements of `V(R)` defined by*

<!-- label: III.XXI.6.6.3 -->

```text
(Оұ*_i, p_j) = Оҙ_{ij},
```

*(whence `s_{Оұ_i}(p_i) = p_i вҲ’ Оұ_i` and `s_{Оұ_i}(p_j) = p_j` for `i вү  j`).*[^XXI-6-1] *Then `Оӣ(в„ӣ)` is the free abelian
group generated by the `p_i`.*

<!-- original page 138 -->

The `p_i` are called the *fundamental weights* corresponding to the system of simple coroots `О”*`.

**Corollary 6.6.4.** *For every `Оұ* вҲҲ О”*`, one has therefore `(Оұ*, ОЈбөў p_i) = 1`, hence `ОЈбөў p_i = ПҒ_{RвӮҠ}` (cf. 3.5.1),
where `RвӮҠ = P(ind(О”))`.*

<!-- label: III.XXI.6.6.4 -->

**Corollary 6.6.5.** *For every `x вҲҲ V(R)`, one has `x = ОЈбөў (Оұ*_i, x) p_i`.*

<!-- label: III.XXI.6.6.5 -->

Remark that `R* вҠӮ О“вӮҖ(R*)` and `R вҠӮ Оӣ(в„ӣ)`, so `(Оӣ(в„ӣ), О“вӮҖ(R*), R, R*)` is a root datum.

**Corollary 6.6.6.** *The canonical morphism `О“вӮҖ(R*) вҶ’ M*` is the transpose of the morphism `x вҶҰ ОЈбөў (Оұ*_i, x) p_i`,
which defines a morphism of root data, and one has a commutative diagram:*

<!-- label: III.XXI.6.6.6 -->

```text
              sc(в„ӣ)
            вҶ—   вүҖ
       в„ӣ
            вҶҳ
              (Оӣ(в„ӣ), О“вӮҖ(R*), R, R*).
```

One thus has an explicit description of `sc(в„ӣ)` in terms of the weights of `в„ӣ`. Similarly, one finds a commutative
diagram:

```text
       ad(в„ӣ)
        вүҖ  вҶҳ
              в„ӣ
            вҶ—
       (О“вӮҖ(R), Оӣ(в„ӣ*), R, R*).
```

**Corollary 6.6.7.** *For `в„ӣ` to be simply connected, it is necessary and sufficient that `M = Оӣ(в„ӣ)`.*

<!-- label: III.XXI.6.6.7 -->

<!-- original page 139 -->

**Remark 6.6.8.** *One has `Оӣ(в„ӣ) вҲ© M = V(R) вҲ© M`. For `в„ӣ` to be semisimple, it is therefore necessary and sufficient
that `M вҠӮ Оӣ(в„ӣ)`.*

<!-- label: III.XXI.6.6.8 -->

From the results of 6.5 there also follows:

**Corollary 6.6.9.** *For `в„ӣ` to be the product of a simply connected datum by a trivial datum, it is necessary and
sufficient that `M вҠғ Оӣ(в„ӣ)`.*

<!-- label: III.XXI.6.6.9 -->

Consider now the canonical isogeny

```text
f : ad(в„ӣ) вҹ¶ sc(в„ӣ),
```

and set `Z(в„ӣ) = K(f)`. One has `Z(в„ӣ) вүғ Z(sc в„ӣ) вүғ Z(ad в„ӣ)`.

**Corollary 6.6.10.** *One has a canonical isomorphism `Z(в„ӣ) = Оӣ(в„ӣ)/О“вӮҖ(R)`. More precisely, one has an exact sequence
of `W(в„ӣ)`-modules:*

<!-- label: III.XXI.6.6.10 -->

```text
0 вҹ¶ О“вӮҖ(R) вҹ¶ Оӣ(в„ӣ) вҹ¶ Z(в„ӣ) вҹ¶ 0.
```

**Corollary 6.6.11.** *One has a canonical pairing*

<!-- label: III.XXI.6.6.11 -->

```text
Z(в„ӣ*) Г— Z(в„ӣ) вҹ¶ в„ҡ/в„Ө
```

*which puts these groups in duality.*

**Remark 6.6.12.** *One has `Z(в„ӣ Г— в„ӣ') вүғ Z(в„ӣ) Г— Z(в„ӣ')`. Consider in particular simply connected data `в„ӣ_i`,
`i = 1, 2, вҖҰ, n`, and a trivial datum `в„ӣ_0`. Set `в„ӣ = в„ӣ_0 Г— в„ӣ_1 Г— вӢҜ Г— в„ӣ_n`. Let `в„ӣ = (M, M*, R, R*)`,
`в„ӣ_0 = (M_0, M*_0, вҲ…, вҲ…)`. One has*

<!-- label: III.XXI.6.6.12 -->

```text
M/О“вӮҖ(R) вүғ M_0 Г— Z(в„ӣ_1) Г— вӢҜ Г— Z(в„ӣ_n).
```

### 6.7. Automorphisms

<!-- label: III.XXI.6.7 -->

An automorphism of `в„ӣ` is, by 6.1.4, an automorphism of `M`, say `u`, such that `u(R) = R` and `бө—u(R*) = R*`. In
particular, every element `w` of `W(в„ӣ)` defines an automorphism of `в„ӣ`.

<!-- original page 140 -->

**Lemma 6.7.1.** *`W(в„ӣ)` is a normal subgroup of `Aut(в„ӣ)`. More precisely, if `u вҲҲ Aut(в„ӣ)` and `Оұ вҲҲ R`, one has*

<!-- label: III.XXI.6.7.1 -->

```text
u s_Оұ uвҒ»В№ = s_{u(Оұ)}.
```

*Proof.* The proof is the same as that of 1.2.10.

**Proposition 6.7.2.** *Let `О”` be a system of simple roots. Set*

<!-- label: III.XXI.6.7.2 -->

```text
E_О”(в„ӣ) = {u вҲҲ Aut(в„ӣ) | u(О”) = О”}.
```

*Then `Aut(в„ӣ)` is the semidirect product of `W(в„ӣ)` by `E_О”(в„ӣ)`.*

*Proof.* This follows at once from the fact that `W(в„ӣ)` operates simply transitively on the systems of simple roots,
and from the fact that if `О”` is a system of simple roots of `в„ӣ`, then `u(О”)` is a system of simple roots for every
automorphism `u` of `в„ӣ`.

We shall see later a simpler description of `E_О”(в„ӣ)` in the case of reduced and irreducible root data.[^N.D.E-XXI-27]

**Definition 6.7.3.** *One denotes by `Aut_s(в„ӣ)`[^N.D.E-XXI-28] the set of `u вҲҲ Aut(в„ӣ)` such that the following
diagram is commutative:*

<!-- label: III.XXI.6.7.3 -->

```text
       u
в„ӣ в”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”ҖвҶ’ в„ӣ
  вҶҳ       вҶҷ
   rad(в„ӣ).
```

*One writes `E_О”^s(в„ӣ) = E_О”(в„ӣ) вҲ© Aut_s(в„ӣ)`.*

<!-- original page 141 -->

**Remark 6.7.4.** *If `u вҲҲ Aut(в„ӣ)`, one has therefore `u вҲҲ Aut_s(в„ӣ)` if and only if `(u вҲ’ id)(M) вҠӮ V(R)`. In particular
`W(в„ӣ) вҠӮ Aut_s(в„ӣ)`. It follows at once:*

<!-- label: III.XXI.6.7.4 -->

**Proposition 6.7.5.** *The group `Aut_s(в„ӣ)` is the semidirect product of `W(в„ӣ)` by `E_О”^s(в„ӣ)`, for every system of
simple roots `О”`.*

<!-- label: III.XXI.6.7.5 -->

To every automorphism of `в„ӣ` is associated by functoriality an automorphism of `ad(в„ӣ)`. One thus has a canonical
morphism

```text
Aut(в„ӣ) вҹ¶ Aut(ad(в„ӣ)).
```

**Lemma 6.7.6.** *The morphism `Aut_s(в„ӣ) вҶ’ Aut(ad(в„ӣ))` is injective.*

<!-- label: III.XXI.6.7.6 -->

*Proof.* Let `u` be an automorphism of `M` such that `(u вҲ’ id)(M) вҠӮ V(R)` and `бө—u(Оұ*) = Оұ*` for `Оұ* вҲҲ R*`. For every
`x вҲҲ M`, one has

```text
(Оұ*, u(x) вҲ’ x) = (бө—u(Оұ*) вҲ’ Оұ*, x) = 0,
```

so `u(x) вҲ’ x = 0`, by 1.2.5.

**Lemma 6.7.7.** *The group `Aut_s(в„ӣ)` is finite.*

<!-- label: III.XXI.6.7.7 -->

*Proof.* Indeed, it suffices for us to prove that `Aut(в„ӣ)` is finite if `в„ӣ` is adjoint. Since `M` is then generated by
`R`, every automorphism of `в„ӣ` is determined by the permutation of `R` that it defines.

**Remark 6.7.8.** *One sees at once that `Aut(в„ӣ)` (resp. `E_О”(в„ӣ)`) is finite if and only if `rgred(в„ӣ) вҲ’ rgss(в„ӣ) в©Ҫ 1`.*

<!-- label: III.XXI.6.7.8 -->

### 6.8. `p`-morphisms of reduced root data

<!-- label: III.XXI.6.8 -->

<!-- original page 142 -->

In this number, `p` is an integer `> 0` fixed once and for all.

**Definition 6.8.1.** *Let `в„ӣ = (M, M*, R, R*)` and `в„ӣ' = (M', M'*, R', R'*)` be two reduced root data. One says that a
group morphism*

<!-- label: III.XXI.6.8.1 -->

```text
f : M' вҹ¶ M
```

*is a* `p`-morphism *from `в„ӣ'` to `в„ӣ`, if the following conditions are satisfied: there exist a bijection*

```text
u : R вҘІ R'
```

*and a map `q : R вҶ’ {pвҒҝ, n вҲҲ в„•}` such that:*

*(i) `f(u(Оұ)) = q(Оұ) Оұ` for every `Оұ вҲҲ R`.*

*(ii) `бө—f(Оұ'*) = q(Оұ) u(Оұ)*` for every `Оұ вҲҲ R`.*[^N.D.E-XXI-29]

**Corollary 6.8.2.** *A `1`-morphism is nothing other than a morphism.*

<!-- label: III.XXI.6.8.2 -->

**Corollary 6.8.3.** *The transpose of a `p`-morphism is a `p`-morphism.*

<!-- label: III.XXI.6.8.3 -->

**Lemma 6.8.4.** *If `w вҲҲ W(в„ӣ)`, `Оұ вҲҲ R`, one has `q(w(Оұ)) = q(Оұ)`. The map `s_Оұ вҶҰ s_{u(Оұ)}` extends to an isomorphism
`u : W(в„ӣ) вҶ’ W(в„ӣ')` such that*

<!-- label: III.XXI.6.8.4 -->

```text
u(w(Оұ)) = u(w)(u(Оұ)).
```

*Proof.* It suffices to prove that for `Оұ, ОІ вҲҲ R`, one has `u(s_Оұ(ОІ)) = s_{u(Оұ)} u(ОІ)` and `q(s_Оұ(ОІ)) = q(ОІ)`. Now one
has successively:

```text
f(s_{u(Оұ)} u(ОІ)) = f(u(ОІ)) вҲ’ (u(Оұ)*, u(ОІ)) f(u(Оұ))
                = q(ОІ) ОІ вҲ’ q(ОІ) q(Оұ)вҒ»В№ (Оұ*, ОІ) q(Оұ) Оұ
                = q(ОІ) (ОІ вҲ’ (Оұ*, ОІ) Оұ) = q(ОІ) s_Оұ(ОІ).
```

<!-- original page 143 -->

If `Оі = uвҒ»В№(s_{u(Оұ)} u(ОІ))`, one has therefore `q(Оі) Оі = f(u(Оі)) = q(ОІ) s_Оұ(ОІ)`. The two roots `Оі` and `s_Оұ(ОІ)` are
therefore proportional (over `в„ҡ`), so equal or opposite, but `q(Оі)` and `q(ОІ)` are positive. One thus has
`q(Оі) = q(ОІ)` and `Оі = s_Оұ(ОІ)`.

**Definition 6.8.5.** *The `q(Оұ)` are called the* radical exponents *of `f`.*

<!-- label: III.XXI.6.8.5 -->

**Example 6.8.6.** *Let `в„ӣ` be a reduced root datum and `q = pвҒҝ` (`n вҲҲ в„•`). Then the multiplication by `q : M вҶ’ M`,
`x вҶҰ q x` is a `p`-morphism whose radical exponents are all equal to `q` (and `u = id`); one denotes it*

<!-- label: III.XXI.6.8.6 -->

```text
q : в„ӣ вҹ¶ в„ӣ.
```

**Proposition 6.8.7.** *In the notation of 6.8.1, `u` realizes an isomorphism of the set of systems of simple roots
(resp. of positive roots) of `R` onto the corresponding set for `R'`.*

<!-- label: III.XXI.6.8.7 -->

*Proof.* This follows at once from 3.1.5 (resp. 3.2.1).

## 7. Structure

<!-- label: III.XXI.7 -->

<!-- original page 144 -->

### 7.1. Decomposition of a root datum

<!-- label: III.XXI.7.1 -->

**Proposition 7.1.1.** *Let `в„ӣ` be a root datum, `О”` a system of simple roots.*

<!-- label: III.XXI.7.1.1 -->

*(i) Let `R'` and `R''` be two closed and symmetric sets of roots forming a partition of `R`. If one denotes
`О”' = О” вҲ© R'`, `О”'' = О” вҲ© R''`, then `R' = R_{О”'}`, `R'' = R_{О”''}`, and every root of `О”'` is orthogonal to every
root of `О”''`.*

*(ii) Let `О”'` and `О”''` be two subsets of `О”` forming a partition of `О”` and orthogonal. Then `R' = R_{О”'}` and
`R'' = R_{О”''}` form a partition of `R`.*

*Proof.* Let us first prove (i).

**Lemma 7.1.2.** *Under the conditions of (i), if `Оұ`, `ОІ`, and `Оұ + ОІ` are roots, they all three belong to `R'` or
all three to `R''`.*

<!-- label: III.XXI.7.1.2 -->

*Proof.* Suppose for example `Оұ + ОІ вҲҲ R'`. Then one cannot have `Оұ, ОІ вҲҲ R''`, since `R''` is closed; suppose therefore
`Оұ вҲҲ R'`. Then `вҲ’Оұ вҲҲ R'` and `ОІ = (ОІ + Оұ) вҲ’ Оұ вҲҲ R'`.

Let us now show that `R' = R_{О”'}` by induction on the order of a positive root `Оұ вҲҲ R' вҲ© P(О”)`. If `ord_О”(Оұ) = 1`,
then `Оұ вҲҲ R' вҲ© О” = О”'`. If `ord_О”(Оұ) > 1`, there exists `ОІ вҲҲ О”` such that `Оұ вҲ’ ОІ вҲҲ R`. By the lemma, one has `ОІ вҲҲ О”'`,
`Оұ вҲ’ ОІ вҲҲ R'`, so `Оұ вҲ’ ОІ вҲҲ R_{О”'}` by induction, and finally `Оұ = (Оұ вҲ’ ОІ) + ОІ вҲҲ R_{О”'}`.

Let us finally show that `О”'` and `О”''` are orthogonal. If `Оұ вҲҲ О”'` and `ОІ вҲҲ О”''`, then `(ОІ*, Оұ) в©Ҫ 0`. If
`(ОІ*, Оұ) вү  0`, then `ОІ + Оұ` is a root, contrary to the lemma.

Let us prove (ii). If `О”'` or `О”''` is empty, this is immediate. Otherwise, if `R_{О”'}` and `R_{О”''}` do not form a
partition of `R`, there exists a root `Оұ` of the form

<!-- original page 145 -->

```text
Оұ = ОЈ_i m'_i Оұ'_i + ОЈ_j m''_j Оұ''_j,    m'_i вҲҲ в„ӨвӮҠ, m''_j вҲҲ в„ӨвӮҠ,
```

where one denotes by `Оұ'_i` (resp. `Оұ''_j`) elements of `О”'` (resp. `О”''`). Applying 3.1.2, one deduces a relation of
the form (possibly inverting `О”'` and `О”''`):

```text
Оҙ = Оі + ОІ,    Оі вҲҲ R_{О”'},    ОІ вҲҲ О”'',    Оҙ вҲҲ R.
```

But since `(ОІ*, Оі) = 0`, `Оі вҲ’ ОІ` is also a root by 2.2.5, which is impossible.

**Proposition 7.1.3.** *Let `в„ӣ` be a root datum. The following conditions are equivalent:*

<!-- label: III.XXI.7.1.3 -->

*(i) There exists no non-trivial partition of `R` into two closed and symmetric subsets.*

*(ii) For some (resp. every) system of simple roots `О”` of `R`, there exists no partition of `О”` into two non-empty
orthogonal subsets.*

<!-- original page 102 -->

*(iii) The natural representation of `W(в„ӣ)` in `V(R)` is irreducible.*

*(iv) For every pair `(Оұ, ОІ)` of roots, there exists a sequence of roots `Оұ_0, Оұ_1, Оұ_2, вҖҰ, Оұ_n`, with `Оұ = Оұ_0`,
`Оұ_n = ОІ`, such that the roots `Оұ_i` and `Оұ_{i+1}` (`i = 0, вҖҰ, n вҲ’ 1`) are non-orthogonal.*

*Proof.* One has (i) вҮ” (ii) by 7.1.1. One has obviously (iv) вҮ’ (ii). Conversely, if (ii) is verified for `О”`,
condition (iv) is verified for `Оұ, ОІ вҲҲ О”`. Now for every root, there exists a simple root not orthogonal to it (3.1.1
for example). On the other hand (iii) вҮ’ (i): in effect, under the conditions of 7.1.1, `V(R')` is stable under `W(в„ӣ)`.
It remains to prove (i) вҮ’ (iii).

<!-- original page 146 -->

So let `H` be a vector subspace of `V(R)`, stable under `W(в„ӣ)`. For every `Оұ вҲҲ R`, the equation `s_Оұ(H) = H` gives at
once `Оұ вҲҲ H`, or `Оұ* вҲҲ H^вҠҘ` (orthogonal of `H` in `V(R*)`, which is in duality with `V(R)`). If one sets
`R' = {Оұ вҲҲ R | Оұ вҲҲ H}` and `R'' = {Оұ вҲҲ R | Оұ* вҲҲ H^вҠҘ}`, one has realized a partition of `R` into two closed and
symmetric subsets.

**Definition 7.1.4.** *A root datum (resp. a root system) satisfying the equivalent conditions of 7.1.3 and of
semisimple rank `вү  0` is called* irreducible.

<!-- label: III.XXI.7.1.4 -->

**Corollary 7.1.5.** *For every root datum `в„ӣ`, there exists a unique partition (up to order) of `R` into closed,
symmetric, and irreducible subsets.*

<!-- label: III.XXI.7.1.5 -->

**Corollary 7.1.6.** *Every adjoint (resp. simply connected) root datum is a product of irreducible adjoint
(resp. simply connected) root data.*

<!-- label: III.XXI.7.1.6 -->

*Proof.* It suffices to see this in the adjoint case. The assertion then follows from the fact that under the
conditions of 7.1.1, one has

```text
О“вӮҖ(R) = О“вӮҖ(R') Г— О“вӮҖ(R'').
```

**Corollary 7.1.7.** *For every root datum (resp. reduced root datum) `в„ӣ`, there exists an isogeny `в„ӣ вҶ’ в„ӣ'`, where
`в„ӣ'` is a product of a trivial root datum and of irreducible simply connected root data (resp. and reduced).*

<!-- label: III.XXI.7.1.7 -->

### 7.2. Properties of irreducible root data

<!-- label: III.XXI.7.2 -->

<!-- original page 147 -->

**Definition 7.2.1.** *Let `в„ӣ` be an irreducible root datum. For every `Оұ вҲҲ R`, set*

<!-- label: III.XXI.7.2.1 -->

```text
long(Оұ) = в„“(Оұ)/в„“(Оұ_0),
```

*where `Оұ_0 вҲҲ R` is such that `в„“(Оұ_0)` is minimal; one says that `long(Оұ)` is the* length *of `Оұ`.*

**Lemma 7.2.2.** *Let `в„ӣ` be an irreducible root datum. The Weyl group operates transitively on the set of roots of the
same length.*

<!-- label: III.XXI.7.2.2 -->

*Proof.* Indeed, let `Оұ, ОІ вҲҲ R`. Since the representation of `W` on `V(R)` is irreducible, `Оұ` cannot be orthogonal to
all the `w(ОІ)`, `w вҲҲ W`. There exists therefore `w вҲҲ W` with `w(ОІ)` non-orthogonal to `Оұ`. Now `в„“(w(ОІ)) = в„“(ОІ)`, and
one concludes by 2.3.2.

**Lemma 7.2.3.** *If `в„ӣ` is irreducible and reduced, then `long(R)` is `{1}`, `{1, 2}`, or `{1, 3}`.*

<!-- label: III.XXI.7.2.3 -->

<!-- original page 103 -->

*Proof.* By virtue of the remark used above, for every `Оұ, ОІ вҲҲ R`, there always exists `w вҲҲ W` such that `w(ОІ)` is
not orthogonal to `ОІ`. One thus has `в„“(Оұ)/в„“(ОІ) = 1, 2, 3, 1/2`, or `1/3` (by 2.3.1). One has therefore
`long(Оұ) = 1, 2`, or `3`, but if `long(Оұ) = 2`, `long(ОІ) = 3`, then `в„“(Оұ)/в„“(ОІ) = 2/3`, which is impossible.

**Remark 7.2.4.** *Reasoning similarly, one proves the following result: if `в„ӣ` is irreducible and not reduced with
`rgss(в„ӣ) > 1`, one has `long(R) = {1, 2, 4}`. If one sets `longвҒ»В№(i) = R_i`, then `ind(R) = R_1 вҲӘ R_2`, `R_4 = 2 R_1`,
and two non-proportional roots of `R_1` are orthogonal. Conversely, if `R` is an irreducible and reduced system such
that `long(R) = {1, 2}`, set `longвҒ»В№(i) = R_i` and suppose that two non-proportional roots of `R_1` are orthogonal;
then `R вҲӘ 2 R_1` is irreducible, not reduced, and `ind(R вҲӘ 2 R_1) = R`.*

<!-- label: III.XXI.7.2.4 -->

**Lemma 7.2.5.** *If `в„ӣ` is an irreducible root datum, `в„ӣ*` also is, and the product `long(Оұ) long(Оұ*)` is constant as
`Оұ` runs over `R`.*

<!-- label: III.XXI.7.2.5 -->

<!-- original page 148 -->

*Proof.* This follows at once from 7.1.3 (iv) and 2.2.6.

**Definition 7.2.6.** *Let `в„ӣ` be any root datum. One calls* length *of `Оұ вҲҲ R` and denotes by `long(Оұ)` the length of
`Оұ` in its irreducible component.*

<!-- label: III.XXI.7.2.6 -->

**Lemma 7.2.7.** *There exists a unique group homomorphism `u : О“вӮҖ(R) вҶ’ О“вӮҖ(R*)` such that `u(Оұ) = long(Оұ) Оұ*` for
`Оұ вҲҲ R`.*

<!-- label: III.XXI.7.2.7 -->

*Proof.* By 3.5.5, it suffices to verify that if `Оұ, ОІ, Оұ + ОІ вҲҲ R`, one has

```text
long(Оұ) Оұ* + long(ОІ) ОІ* = long(Оұ + ОІ) (Оұ + ОІ)*.
```

But `Оұ`, `ОІ`, and `Оұ + ОІ` are in the same irreducible component of `R` by 7.1.2, and one is reduced to 1.2.2.

**Remark 7.2.8.** *Let `u` be as in 7.2.7. For `Оұ, ОІ вҲҲ R`, one has `(u(Оұ), ОІ) = (u(ОІ), Оұ)`. Indeed, this amounts to
seeing that*

<!-- label: III.XXI.7.2.8 -->

```text
long(Оұ) (Оұ*, ОІ) = long(ОІ) (ОІ*, Оұ),
```

*which is obviously verified if `Оұ` and `ОІ` are orthogonal. If `Оұ` and `ОІ` are not orthogonal, then they are in the
same irreducible component of `R`, and one is reduced to 1.2.1, formula (9).*

**Remark 7.2.9.** *The symmetric bilinear form `(u(x), y)` is positive nondegenerate on `О“вӮҖ(R)`.*

<!-- label: III.XXI.7.2.9 -->

*Proof.* Indeed, let `R_i` be the irreducible components of `R`. One has

```text
О“вӮҖ(R) = вҲҸ_i О“вӮҖ(R_i),
```

and the bilinear form `(u(x), y)` is the product of the forms

```text
2вҒ»В№ в„“(Оұ_i)вҒ»В№ (p(x), y)
```

<!-- original page 149 -->

on the `О“вӮҖ(R_i)`, where `в„“(Оұ_i)` is the minimum of `в„“(Оұ)` for `Оұ вҲҲ R_i`. Now these various symmetric bilinear forms are
positive nondegenerate (1.2.6).

### 7.3. Cartan matrix

<!-- label: III.XXI.7.3 -->

Let `в„ӣ` be a root datum. If `О”` is a system of simple roots, one calls *Cartan matrix* of `в„ӣ` relative to `О”` the
square matrix on the index set `О”` defined by

```text
a_{Оұ,ОІ} = (Оұ*, ОІ),    for Оұ, ОІ вҲҲ О”.
```

Let us first remark that if `О”'` is another system of simple roots and `w` an element of `W(в„ӣ)` such that `w(О”) = О”'`,
one has

```text
(w(Оұ)*, w(ОІ)) = (Оұ*, ОІ),
```

so the Cartan matrix of `в„ӣ` relative to `О”'` is obtained from that relative to `О”` by the isomorphism `О” вҶ’ О”'` on the
index set defined by `w`. It follows that, up to isomorphism on the index set, the Cartan matrix depends only on `в„ӣ`.

**Proposition 7.3.1.** *The Cartan matrix has the following properties:*

<!-- label: III.XXI.7.3.1 -->

*(i) `a_{Оұ,Оұ} = 2`, `a_{Оұ,ОІ} в©Ҫ 0` for `Оұ вү  ОІ`.*

*(ii) `a_{Оұ,ОІ} = 0` entails `a_{ОІ,Оұ} = 0`.*

*(iii) There exist strictly positive integers `m_Оұ` (`= long(Оұ)`) such that the matrix*

```text
(m_Оұ a_{Оұ,ОІ})
```

*is symmetric, positive, and nondegenerate.*

<!-- original page 150 -->

*(iv) The diagonal minors of the matrix `(a_{Оұ,ОІ})_{Оұ, ОІ вҲҲ О”}`, i.e. the determinants*

```text
det(a_{Оұ,ОІ})_{Оұ, ОІ вҲҲ О”'}    for О”' вҠӮ О”,
```

*are strictly positive.*

*(v) One has `s_Оұ(ОІ) = ОІ вҲ’ a_{Оұ,ОІ} Оұ` and `s_Оұ(ОІ*) = ОІ* вҲ’ a_{ОІ,Оұ} Оұ*`.*

*Proof.* Indeed, (v) is a definition, (i) follows from 3.2.11, (ii) from 2.2.2, (iii) from 7.2.9, (iv) follows at
once from (iii) by the relation

```text
det(m_Оұ a_{Оұ,ОІ})_{Оұ, ОІ вҲҲ О”'} = (вҲҸ_{Оұ вҲҲ О”'} m_Оұ) det(a_{Оұ,ОІ})_{Оұ, ОІ вҲҲ О”'}.
```

**Proposition 7.3.2.** *Let `в„ӣ` and `в„ӣ'` be two simply connected (resp. adjoint) reduced root data, `О”` (resp. `О”'`) a
system of simple roots of `в„ӣ` (resp. `в„ӣ'`), and `u : О” вҶ’ О”'` an isomorphism such that if one denotes by `(a_{Оұ,ОІ})`
and `(a'_{Оұ',ОІ'})` the Cartan matrices of `в„ӣ` and `в„ӣ'` relative to `О”` and `О”'`, one has:*

<!-- label: III.XXI.7.3.2 -->

```text
a'_{u(Оұ), u(ОІ)} = a_{Оұ,ОІ}.
```

*Then there exists a unique isomorphism of `в„ӣ` onto `в„ӣ'` that induces `u` on `О”`.*

*Proof.* It evidently suffices to make the proof in the adjoint case. Then `M = О“вӮҖ(R)` and `M' = О“вӮҖ(R')` are the free
abelian groups generated by `О”` and `О”'`. There exists therefore a unique isomorphism of groups of `M` onto `M'` that
induces `u` on `О”`. Denote it also `u`. Let us show that `u(R) вҠӮ R'`. Every root `Оұ` of `R` is written
`s_{Оұ_1} вӢҜ s_{Оұ_n}(Оұ_{n+1})` with `Оұ_i вҲҲ О”`. One has obviously

```text
u(Оұ) = s_{u(Оұ_1)} вӢҜ s_{u(Оұ_n)}(u(Оұ_{n+1})),
```

<!-- original page 151 -->

by virtue of the hypothesis on `u` and the relations (v) of 7.3.1.

It remains to prove that `бө—u(R'*) вҠӮ R*`, which follows from the fact that the elements of `M*` (resp. `M'*`) are
determined by the duality with `R` or `О”` (resp. `R'` or `О”'`), by 1.2.5.

**Corollary 7.3.3.** *A reduced simply connected or adjoint root datum is determined up to isomorphism by its Cartan
matrix.*

<!-- label: III.XXI.7.3.3 -->

**Corollary 7.3.4.** *Let `в„ӣ` be a reduced simply connected (resp. adjoint) root datum, and `О”` a system of simple
roots. The group `E_О”(в„ӣ)` is identified with the group of automorphisms of the set `О”` that leave the Cartan matrix
invariant.*

<!-- label: III.XXI.7.3.4 -->

**Remark 7.3.5.** *The question of the existence of a root datum corresponding to a given Cartan matrix satisfying
(i), (ii), and (iv) (for example) is not easily resolved directly, without using the classification.*

### 7.4. Dynkin diagrams

<!-- label: III.XXI.7.4 -->

**Definition 7.4.1.** *One calls a* Dynkin diagram structure *(the word "schГ©ma" having been banished for obvious
reasons) on a finite set `О”` the data of a set of pairs of distinct elements of `О”`, called* linked pairs, *and of a
map from `О”` to the set `{1, 2, 3}`. The notion of isomorphism of such structures is obvious.*

<!-- label: III.XXI.7.4.1 -->

**Definition 7.4.2.** *Let `в„ӣ` be a root datum and `О”` a system of simple roots. One calls* Dynkin diagram *of `в„ӣ`
relative to `О”` the set `О”`, two simple roots being linked if and only if they are non-orthogonal, with each root
being assigned its length.*

<!-- label: III.XXI.7.4.2 -->

<!-- original page 152 -->

**Proposition 7.4.3.** *Dynkin diagram and Cartan matrix determine each other biuniquely.*

<!-- label: III.XXI.7.4.3 -->

*Proof.* Indeed, the equivalence

```text
Оұ and ОІ are not linked  вҹә  a_{Оұ,ОІ} = 0,
```

and the relation

```text
long(Оұ) a_{Оұ,ОІ} = long(ОІ) a_{ОІ,Оұ},
```

(with `inf long(Оұ) = 1` in each connected component of the diagram) determine the `a_{Оұ,ОІ}` as a function of the
linkages and lengths, and conversely (the details of the verification are left to the reader).

**Corollary 7.4.4.** *A reduced simply connected or adjoint root datum is determined by its Dynkin diagram.*

<!-- label: III.XXI.7.4.4 -->

**Corollary 7.4.5.** *Let `в„ӣ` be a reduced simply connected or adjoint root datum and `О”` a system of simple roots.
The group `E_О”(в„ӣ)` is identified with the group of automorphisms of the Dynkin diagram of `в„ӣ` relative to `О”`, that is
to say with the group of permutations of `О”` preserving lengths and linkages.*

<!-- label: III.XXI.7.4.5 -->

**Remark 7.4.6.** *One classifies, with the usual method,[^XXI-7-1][^N.D.E-XXI-30] the various connected Dynkin

### 7.5. Complements on `p`-morphisms

<!-- label: III.XXI.7.5 -->

Let `f : в„ӣ вҶ’ в„ӣ'` be a `p`-morphism (cf. 6.8). It is clear from the definitions that the bijection `u : R вҘІ R'`
associated with `f` makes systems of simple roots, systems of positive roots, irreducible components (etc.) of `R` and
`R'` correspond. Suppose therefore, for simplicity, `R` and `R'` irreducible.

**Lemma 7.5.1.** *If `R` and `R'` are irreducible, there exists `k вҲҲ в„ҡ` such that for every `Оұ вҲҲ R`*

<!-- label: III.XXI.7.5.1 -->

```text

```

<!-- original page 154 -->

*Proof.* Indeed, one has `long(Оұ) (Оұ*, ОІ) = long(ОІ) (ОІ*, Оұ)` and, similarly,

```text
long(u(Оұ)) (u(Оұ)*, u(ОІ)) = long(u(ОІ)) (u(ОІ)*, u(Оұ)).
```

```text
q(Оұ)ВІ long(Оұ) / long(u(Оұ)) = q(ОІ)ВІ long(ОІ) / long(u(ОІ))
```

of the preceding number shows that there are four possible cases (we denote by the same letter a Dynkin diagram and the
