# ExposГ© XXV. The existence theorem

<!-- label: III.XXV -->

*by M. Demazure*

<!-- original page 269 -->

[^N.D.E-XXV-0] For the sake of completeness, we give in this ExposГ© a proof of the existence theorem for split groups.
Like Chevalley's original proof ("Sur certains schГ©mas de groupes semi-simples", *SГ©minaire Bourbaki*, May 1961, no. 219),
it rests on the existence of complex semisimple algebraic groups of all possible types. The principle of a more
satisfactory proof, establishing directly the existence of a simply connected split semisimple `в„Ө`-group corresponding
to a given Cartan matrix, was given by Cartier (unpublished).[^N.D.E-XXV-1] Let us point out, however, that the
difficulty is not to give an explicit construction of a group scheme, but to verify that the group thus constructed
really meets the required conditions, that is, essentially, that its fibers are indeed smooth and reductive.

## 1. Statement of the theorem

<!-- label: III.XXV.1 -->

**Theorem 1.1.** *Let `S` be a non-empty scheme. The functor*

```text
G вҶҰ R(G)
```

*is an equivalence of the category of pinned `S`-reductive groups with the category of pinned reduced root data.*

<!-- label: III.XXV.1.1 -->

<!-- original page 269 (cont., page 411 of original) -->

By virtue of the uniqueness theorem (Exp. XXIII, 4.1), the preceding statement is equivalent to:

**Corollary 1.2 (Existence of split groups).** *For every reduced root datum `R`, there exists a split reductive
`в„Ө`-group `G` such that `R(G) вүғ R`.*[^N.D.E-XXV-2]

<!-- label: III.XXV.1.2 -->

<!-- original page 270 -->

In particular:

**Corollary 1.3.** *Let `k` be a field. For every splittable reductive `k`-group `G_k`, there exists a reductive
`в„Ө`-group `G` such that `G вҠ—_в„Ө k вүғ G_k`.*

<!-- label: III.XXV.1.3 -->

Conversely, let us remark first that to prove 1.2 it suffices, by Exp. XXII 4.3.1 and Exp. XXI 6.5.10, to consider the
case where the root datum `R` is simply connected (and even irreducible if one insists, by Exp. XXI, 7.1.6).

On the other hand, under the conditions of 1.3, the group scheme `G` is of constant type (since `Spec(в„Ө)` is connected),
hence of type `R(G_k)`; by Exp. XXIII, 5.9, it follows that the validity of 1.3 for a given group `G_k` entails the
existence of a split `в„Ө`-group of type `R(G_k)`.[^N.D.E-XXV-3]

To prove 1.2, and hence 1.1, it therefore suffices to prove 1.3 when `k` is of characteristic zero (for example
`k = в„Ӯ`) and `G_k` is simply connected (and in particular semisimple), as well as:

**Proposition 1.4.** *For every simply connected reduced root datum `R`, there exists a semisimple algebraic `в„Ӯ`-group
of type `R`.*

<!-- label: III.XXV.1.4 -->

One may prove 1.4 in the following way. One knows first that there exists a complex semisimple Lie algebra `g` of
type `R`, cf. for example N. Jacobson, *Lie Algebras*, ch. VII, Th. 5.[^N.D.E-XXV-4] Then `G = Aut(g)вҒ°` is a semisimple
algebraic `в„Ӯ`-group of type `ad(R)`.[^N.D.E-XXV-5] By *Bible*, В§ 23.1, Prop. 1, one deduces from this the existence of a
semisimple `в„Ӯ`-group of type `R`.

The rest of this ExposГ© is devoted to the proof of 1.3 for `k` of characteristic zero and `G_k` semisimple. It will be
carried out in two stages: construction of a "piece of `в„Ө`-group scheme" (nВ° 2), and study of the group obtained by
application of the "theorem of Weil" (nВ° 3).

<!-- original page 271 -->

To avoid confusion, we shall not use in nВ° 2 the abbreviated notation `X_k` to denote the `k`-scheme `X вҠ—_в„Ө k`, where
`X` is a `в„Ө`-scheme.

## 2. Existence theorem: construction of a piece of group

<!-- label: III.XXV.2 -->

### 2.1.

<!-- label: III.XXV.2.1 -->

Let us choose, once and for all, a splitting of `G_k`, denoted

```text
(G_k, T_k, M, R)
```

(cf. Exp. XXII, 1.13), a system of simple roots `О”` of `R` (defining the system of positive roots `RвҒә`), a Chevalley
system `(X_{Оұ,k})_{ОұвҲҲR}` of `G_k` (Exp. XXIII, 6.1 and 6.2) satisfying the following supplementary condition (cf. XX 2.6):
for every `Оұ вҲҲ R`, one has

```text
X_{Оұ,k} X_{вҲ’Оұ,k} = 1.
```

Finally, let us choose on the subgroup of `M` generated by `R` a total order relation compatible with the group
structure, such that the roots `> 0` are the elements of `RвҒә`. We then denote the roots

```text
вҲ’Оұ_n < вҲ’Оұ_{nвҲ’1} < вӢҜ < вҲ’Оұ_1 < Оұ_1 < Оұ_2 < вӢҜ < Оұ_n.
```

For `Оұ вҲҲ R`, we denote by `U_{Оұ,k}` the vector group corresponding to the root `Оұ`, and by

```text
p_{Оұ,k} : рқ”ҫ_{a,k} вҘІ U_{Оұ,k}
```

the isomorphism of vector groups defined by `X_{Оұ,k}`.

### 2.2.

<!-- label: III.XXV.2.2 -->

The splitting of `G_k` includes in particular an isomorphism of `k`-groups

```text
T_k вүғ D_k(M).
```

Put `T = D(M)`; this is a `в„Ө`-torus, and the previous isomorphism may be regarded as an isomorphism

```text
T_k вүғ T вҠ—_в„Ө k.
```

<!-- original page 271 (cont., page 413 of original) -->

One has

```text
Hom_{в„Ө-gr.}(T, рқ”ҫ_m) = M,
```

and one will regard the elements of `R вҠӮ M` as characters of `T`. Likewise, one will regard the elements of `R*` as
morphisms of `в„Ө`-groups `рқ”ҫ_m вҶ’ T`.

### 2.3.

<!-- label: III.XXV.2.3 -->

For each `Оұ вҲҲ RвҒә`, let `рқ”ҫ_a(Оұ)` be a copy of the group `рқ”ҫ_a`; consider the `в„Ө`-scheme

```text
U = рқ”ҫ_a(Оұ_1) Г— вӢҜ Г— рқ”ҫ_a(Оұ_n).
```

If `U_k` denotes the unipotent part of the Borel group `B_k` of `G_k` defined by `RвҒә`, denote by

```text
a : U вҠ—_в„Ө k вҘІ U_k
```

the isomorphism of `k`-schemes defined by

```text
a(x_1, вҖҰ, x_n) = p_{Оұ_1,k}(x_1) вӢҜ p_{Оұ_n,k}(x_n).
```

### 2.4.

<!-- label: III.XXV.2.4 -->

<!-- original page 272 -->

The group law of `U_k` translates into relations of the form

```text
a(x_1, вҖҰ, x_n) В· a(y_1, вҖҰ, y_n) = a(z_1, вҖҰ, z_n),
```

where each `z_h` (`h = 1, вҖҰ, n`) is expressed as a polynomial

```text
z_h = x_h + y_h + Q_h(x_1, вҖҰ, x_{hвҲ’1}, y_1, вҖҰ, y_{hвҲ’1}),
```

the coefficients of `Q_h` being integers (Exp. XXII, 5.5.8 and Exp. XXIII, 6.4). Moreover
`Q_h(x_1, вҖҰ, x_{hвҲ’1}, 0, вҖҰ, 0) = 0`.

<!-- original page 272 (cont., page 414 of original) -->

Let us equip `U` with the composition law defined by the preceding formulas (which are indeed "defined over `в„Ө`"). Since
this law induces on `U_k` its group law, it is associative, and `(0)` is a unit element (indeed, the two preceding
assertions are expressed by relations between the polynomials `Q_h`, and `в„Ө вҶ’ k` is injective). Let us show that this is
a group law: if `(x_i)` is a section of `U` (over some `S`), one computes the inverse `(y_i)` of `(x_i)` by the
recurrence formulas

```text
y_i = вҲ’x_i вҲ’ Q_i(x_1, вҖҰ, x_{iвҲ’1}, y_1, вҖҰ, y_{iвҲ’1})
```

which are still "defined over `в„Ө`".

In summary, we have constructed on `U` a group law such that the previous isomorphism `a` is an isomorphism of groups.

For each `Оұ вҲҲ RвҒә`, consider the morphism

```text
p_Оұ : рқ”ҫ_a вҶ’ U
```

defined by `p_Оұ(x) = (x_i)` where

```text
x_i = x  if Оұ_i = Оұ,
x_i = 0  if Оұ_i вү  Оұ.
```

This is a closed immersion and a group homomorphism; we denote its image by `U_Оұ`. One has
`(x_i) = p_{Оұ_1}(x_1) вӢҜ p_{Оұ_n}(x_n)`, which proves that `U` is identified with the product

```text
U = U_{Оұ_1} В· U_{Оұ_2} вӢҜ U_{Оұ_n}.
```

### 2.5.

<!-- label: III.XXV.2.5 -->

Let `T = D(M)` act on each `U_Оұ` through the character `Оұ`; one verifies at once that this defines an action of `T` on
the group `U`, and one can construct the semi-direct product `B = T В· U`. One has a canonical isomorphism of `k`-groups

```text
B вҠ—_в„Ө k вҘІ B_k.
```

<!-- original page 272 (cont., page 415 of original) -->

If we now take any order on `RвҒә`, the morphism

```text
вҲҸ_{ОұвҲҲRвҒә} U_Оұ вҶ’ U
```

defined by the product in `U` is still an isomorphism. Indeed, since both sides are flat `в„Ө`-schemes of finite
presentation, it suffices to verify the assertion on geometric fibers; one is then reduced to Lazard's theory
(*Bible*, В§ 13.1, Prop. 1): one considers `U` as a group with operators `T`, and one uses the fact that the `U_Оұ` are
pairwise non-isomorphic as groups with operators (since the characters `Оұ вҲҲ RвҒә` of `T` are pairwise distinct on each
fiber).

### 2.6.

<!-- label: III.XXV.2.6 -->

<!-- original page 273 -->

Replacing `RвҒә` by `RвҒ» = вҲ’RвҒә`, one constructs similarly the groups `UвҒ»`, `BвҒ»`, `U_Оұ` (`Оұ вҲҲ RвҒ»`) and the isomorphisms

```text
p_Оұ : рқ”ҫ_a вҘІ U_Оұ,  Оұ вҲҲ RвҒ».
```

Let us finally introduce the product scheme

```text
О© = UвҒ» Г— T Г— U;
```

one has a canonical isomorphism of `k`-schemes

```text
О© вҠ—_в„Ө k вҘІ UвҒ»_k Г—_k T_k Г—_k U_k вүғ О©_k,
```

where `О©_k` is the "big cell" of `G_k` (Exp. XXII, 4.1.2).

From now on, we identify `О© вҠ—_в„Ө k` with `О©_k` by the previous isomorphism; we regard `UвҒ»`, `T`, `U` as subschemes of
`О©`, through their unit sections. We denote by `e = ((0), e, (0))` the "unit section" of `О©`.

Our goal now is to put a piece-of-group law on `О©`.

**Lemma 2.7.** *Let `Оұ вҲҲ О”`, and let `w_{Оұ,k}` be the element of `Norm_{G_k}(T_k)(k)` defined by `X_{Оұ,k}` (recall that
by definition*

```text
w_{Оұ,k} = p_{вҲ’Оұ,k}(вҲ’1) p_{Оұ,k}(1) p_{вҲ’Оұ,k}(вҲ’1)).
```

*There exist an open subset `V_Оұ` of `О©` containing the section `e`, and a morphism*

```text
h_Оұ : V_Оұ вҶ’ О©,
```

*satisfying the following conditions:*

*(i) `h_Оұ(e) = e`,*

*(ii) `(h_Оұ) вҠ—_в„Ө k` coincides with the restriction of `int(w_{Оұ,k})` to `V_Оұ вҠ—_в„Ө k вҠӮ G_k`.*

*(iii) One has `T вҠӮ V_Оұ`, and `h_Оұ` sends `T` into `T`. For every `ОІ вҲҲ R`, one has `U_ОІ вҠӮ V_Оұ` and `h_Оұ` sends `U_ОІ`
into `U_{s_Оұ(ОІ)}`.*

<!-- label: III.XXV.2.7 -->

<!-- original page 273 (cont., page 416 of original) -->

By virtue of the definition of a Chevalley system (Exp. XXIII, 6.1), there exists for each `ОІ вҲҲ R` an integer
`e_ОІ = Вұ1` such that

```text
int(w_{Оұ,k}) p_{ОІ,k}(x) = p_{s_Оұ(ОІ),k}(e_ОІ x)
```

for every `x вҲҲ рқ”ҫ_a(S)`, `S вҶ’ Spec(k)`.

Let `S` be any scheme, and write an arbitrary element of `О©(S)` in the form

```text
u = ( вҲҸ_{ОІвҲҲRвҒ», ОІвү вҲ’Оұ} p_ОІ(x_ОІ) В· p_{вҲ’Оұ}(x_{вҲ’Оұ}),  t,  p_Оұ(x_Оұ) В· вҲҸ_{ОІвҲҲRвҒә, ОІвү Оұ} p_ОІ(x_ОІ) ),
```

where one has chosen some (arbitrary) order on `RвҒ» вҲ’ {вҲ’Оұ}` and `RвҒә вҲ’ {Оұ}` (cf. 2.5).

One defines a morphism `d : О© вҶ’ Spec(в„Ө)` by

```text
d(u) = Оұ(t) + x_Оұ x_{вҲ’Оұ}.
```

<!-- original page 273 (cont., page 417 of original) -->

<!-- original page 274 -->

Let `V_Оұ` be the open subset `О©_d` (that is, the open subset of `О©` defined by "`d(u)` invertible"); it contains `e`,
`T`, and each `U_ОІ`, `ОІ вҲҲ R`. Let

```text
h_Оұ : V_Оұ вҶ’ О©
```

be the morphism defined by `h_Оұ(u) = (a(u), b(u), c(u))` where

```text
a(u) = ( вҲҸ_{ОІвҲҲRвҒ», ОІвү вҲ’Оұ} p_{s_Оұ(ОІ)}(e_ОІ x_ОІ) ) В· p_{вҲ’Оұ}(вҲ’x_Оұ d(u)вҒ»В№),

b(u) = t В· Оұ*(d(u)),

c(u) = p_Оұ(вҲ’x_{вҲ’Оұ} d(u)вҒ»В№) В· ( вҲҸ_{ОІвҲҲRвҒә, ОІвү Оұ} p_{s_Оұ(ОІ)}(e_ОІ x_ОІ) ).
```

Since `s_Оұ` permutes the positive roots (resp. negative roots) distinct from `Оұ` (resp. `вҲ’Оұ`), `c(u)` (resp. `a(u)`) is
a section of `U` (resp. `UвҒ»`) and the previous morphism is well-defined. It satisfies (i) and (iii) trivially. As for
(ii), this follows at once from the definition of the `e_ОІ`, `ОІ вҲҲ R`, and from Exp. XX, 3.12.

**Lemma 2.8.** *There exist open subsets `V` and `VвҖІ` of `О©` and morphisms*

```text
h : V вҶ’ О©,  hвҖІ : VвҖІ вҶ’ О©,
```

*satisfying the following conditions:*

*(i) `V` and `VвҖІ` contain `e`, and `h(e) = hвҖІ(e) = e`.*

*(ii) The morphism induced by `hвҖІ вҲҳ h : hвҒ»В№(VвҖІ) вҶ’ О©` is the restriction of the identity morphism.*

*(iii) The `k`-morphisms `h вҠ—_в„Ө k` and `hвҖІ вҠ—_в„Ө k` are the restrictions to `V вҠ—_в„Ө k` and `VвҖІ вҠ—_в„Ө k` of automorphisms of
the group `G_k`.*

*(iv) `V` and `VвҖІ` contain `U`, `T` and `UвҒ»`; `h` and `hвҖІ` send `U` into `UвҒ»`, `UвҒ»` into `U`, and `T` into `T`.*

<!-- label: III.XXV.2.8 -->

<!-- original page 274 (cont., page 418 of original) -->

Let `wвҒ°` be the element of the Weyl group of `G_k` that transforms `RвҒә` into `RвҒ»`. Write

```text
wвҒ° = s_{Оұ_n} вӢҜ s_{Оұ_1},  Оұ_i вҲҲ О”
```

(no relation to the numbering of 2.1). Put

```text
w_0 = w_{Оұ_n,k} вӢҜ w_{Оұ_1,k} вҲҲ Norm_{G_k}(T_k)(k).
```

Define by recursion on `i вүӨ n` an open subset `V_i` of `О©` and a morphism `h_i : V_i вҶ’ О©` by `V_0 = О©`, `h_0 = id`, and,
for `i = 0, вҖҰ, n вҲ’ 1`,

```text
V_{i+1} = h_iвҒ»В№(V_{Оұ_{i+1}}),  h_{i+1} = h_{Оұ_{i+1}} вҲҳ h_i,
```

where the notations `V_{Оұ_j}` and `h_{Оұ_j}` are those of 2.7.

<!-- original page 275 -->

Take `V = V_n` and `h = h_n`. The conditions in (i), (iii) and (iv) bearing on `V` and `h` are indeed verified; for (i)
and (iii) this follows at once from 2.8, for (iv) from the fact that `h вҠ—_в„Ө k` is the restriction of `int(w_0)` to
`V вҠ—_в„Ө k`.

Since `(wвҒ°)ВІ = 1`, one also has

```text

```

Setting

```text
wвҖІ_0 = (w_{Оұ_1,k})Ві вӢҜ (w_{Оұ_n,k})Ві,
```

<!-- original page 275 (cont., page 419 of original) -->

and carrying out the same construction as above, one deduces from it an open subset `VвҖІ` and a morphism `hвҖІ` also
satisfying (i), (iii), (iv). Moreover, `hвҖІ вҠ—_в„Ө k` is the restriction of `int(wвҖІ_0)` to `VвҖІ вҠ—_в„Ө k`. But for each simple
root `Оұ вҲҲ О”`, one has `(w_{Оұ,k})вҒҙ = e` (cf. Exp. XX, 3.1), hence `wвҖІ_0 В· w_0 = e`, which shows that `hвҖІ вҲҳ h` induces the
identity morphism on a non-empty open subset of `О© вҠ—_в„Ө k`. But since `О©` is smooth and of finite presentation over `в„Ө`,
`О© вҠ—_в„Ө k` is schematically dense in `О©`, which proves (ii).

**Proposition 2.9.** *There exist an open subset `V_1` of `О© Г— О©`, an open subset `V_2` of `О©`, and morphisms*

```text
ПҖ : V_1 вҶ’ О©,  Пғ : V_2 вҶ’ О©,
```

*having the following properties:*

*(i) If `x вҲҲ О©(S)`, then `(e, x)` and `(x, e)` are sections of `V_1`, and*

```text
ПҖ(e, x) = ПҖ(x, e) = x.
```

*(ii) `V_2` contains `e` and `Пғ(e) = e`.*

*(iii) `ПҖ_k` and `Пғ_k` are the restrictions of the morphisms `G_k Г—_k G_k вҶ’ G_k` and `G_k вҶ’ G_k` defined by the product
(resp. the inverse).*

*Proof.* Let `(v, t, u)` and `(vвҖІ, tвҖІ, uвҖІ)` be two sections of `О©`. Then `h(u)` is a section of `UвҒ»`, `h(vвҖІ)` is a
section of `U` by 2.8 (iv), and one can therefore regard `(h(u), e, h(vвҖІ))` as a section of `О©`. Let `V_1` be the open
subset of `О© Г— О©` defined by the condition:

```text
((v, t, u), (vвҖІ, tвҖІ, uвҖІ)) вҲҲ V_1(S) вҹә (h(u), e, h(vвҖІ)) вҲҲ VвҖІ(S)
```

(notation of 2.8). If `((v, t, u), (vвҖІ, tвҖІ, uвҖІ))` is a section of `V_1`, then `hвҖІ(h(u), e, h(vвҖІ))` is defined; it is a
section of `О©` which one can decompose:


```text

```

The verification of (i) is immediate (by 2.8 (ii)). To verify the condition in (iii) bearing on `ПҖ`, one sees that

when `u вҲҲ U(S)`, `v вҲҲ UвҒ»(S)`, `S вҶ’ k`, by virtue of 2.8 (iii) and (ii). One constructs `Пғ` similarly: if `(v, t, u)` is a

**Corollary 2.10.** *`ПҖ` is "generically associative" and `Пғ` is a "generic inverse": if `x, y, z вҲҲ О©(S)` and if the
expressions below are defined (which always happens over an open subset of `О©` containing the unit section), one has:*

```text

```

Indeed, the two sides of each of these formulas define morphisms between smooth `в„Ө`-schemes of finite presentation,
which coincide on the generic fibers, by 2.10 (iii).

**Corollary 2.11.** *Let `Оұ вҲҲ R`. For all `S` and all `x, y вҲҲ рқ”ҫ_a(S)` such that `(p_Оұ(x), p_{вҲ’Оұ}(y)) вҲҲ V_1(S)` and
`1 + xy вҲҲ рқ”ҫ_m(S)` (which defines an open subset of `рқ”ҫВІ_{a,S}` containing the section `(0, 0)`), one has:*

```text
ПҖ(p_Оұ(x), p_{вҲ’Оұ}(y)) = ( p_{вҲ’Оұ}( y / (1 + xy) ), Оұ*(1 + xy), p_Оұ( x / (1 + xy) ) ).
```

<!-- label: III.XXV.2.11 -->

The proof is the same as before, by Exp. XX, 2.1.

## 3. Existence theorem: end of the proof

<!-- label: III.XXV.3 -->

To simplify language, let us pose the following definition.

**Definition 3.1.** *Let `S` be a scheme and `G` an `S`-group scheme. One says that `G` is* admissible *if there exists
an open immersion of `S`-schemes `i : О©_S = О© Г— S вҶ’ G` satisfying the following conditions:*

*(i) The diagram*

```text
(V_1)_S в”Җв”Җi Г—_S iв”Җв”Җв–¶ О©_S Г—_S О©_S в”Җв”Җi Г—_S iв”Җв”Җв–¶ G Г—_S G
   в”Ӯ                                              в”Ӯ
   ПҖ                                              ПҖ_G
   в”Ӯ                                              в”Ӯ
   в–ј          в”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җ i_S в”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв–¶        в–ј
  О©_S                                             G,
```

*where `ПҖ_G` denotes the multiplication morphism in `G`, is commutative.*

*(ii) There exists a finite set of sections `a_j вҲҲ О©(S)` such that the `i(a_j) В· i(О©_S)` cover `G`.*

<!-- label: III.XXV.3.1 -->

<!-- original page 276 (cont., page 422 of original) -->

By Weil's "theorem" (Exp. XVIII, 3.13 (iii) and (iv)), one has:

**Lemma 3.2.** *If for every scheme `S` Г©tale and of finite type over `в„Ө`, every admissible `S`-group is affine, then
there exists an admissible affine `в„Ө`-group.*

<!-- label: III.XXV.3.2 -->

<!-- original page 277 -->

Now one has:

**Lemma 3.3.** *Let `S` be a scheme and `G` an admissible `S`-group. Then `G` is smooth and of finite presentation over
`S`, with affine connected semisimple fibers.*

<!-- label: III.XXV.3.3 -->

Since `О©_S` is smooth and of finite presentation over `S`, with connected fibers, the same holds for `G`, by condition
(ii). To verify 3.3, one may therefore suppose that `S` is the spectrum of a field `K`. Let us identify `О©_K` with its
image in `G`. It is clear that `О©_K` is the product

```text
вҲҸ_{ОұвҲҲRвҒ»} U_{Оұ,K} В· T_K В· вҲҸ_{ОұвҲҲRвҒә} U_{Оұ,K}
```

of the subgroups `T_K` and `U_{Оұ,K}` (`Оұ вҲҲ R`) of `G`. The Lie algebra of `G` is therefore identified with the direct
sum

```text
Lie(T_K) вҠ• вЁҒ_{ОұвҲҲR} Lie(U_{Оұ,K}).
```

Since the inner automorphism defined by a section of `T_K` acts on `U_{Оұ,K}`, and therefore on `Lie(U_{Оұ,K})`, through
the character

```text
Оұ вҲҲ R вҠӮ M вүғ Hom_{K-gr.}(T_K, рқ”ҫ_{m,K}),
```

the previous decomposition of `Lie(G)` is exactly the decomposition under the adjoint action of `T`. The roots of `G_K`
with respect to `T_K` are therefore the `Оұ вҲҲ R`. Apply Exp. XIX, 1.13. Let `T_Оұ` be the maximal torus of
`Ker(Оұ) вҠӮ T_K`, and let `Z_Оұ = Centr_G(T_Оұ)`; it suffices for us to prove that each `Z_Оұ` is reductive. Now
`Z_Оұ вҲ© О©_K` is none other than

```text
вҲҸ_{ОІвҲҲRвҒ», ОІ|_{T_Оұ} = e} U_{ОІ,K} В· T_K В· вҲҸ_{ОІвҲҲRвҒә, ОІ|_{T_Оұ} = e} U_{ОІ,K};
```

<!-- original page 277 (cont., page 423 of original) -->

but the roots vanishing on `T_Оұ` are the rational multiples of `Оұ`, hence `Оұ` and `вҲ’Оұ`; this proves

```text
Z_Оұ вҲ© О©_K = U_{вҲ’Оұ,K} В· T_K В· U_{Оұ,K}.
```

To prove that `Z_Оұ` is reductive, it suffices, by Exp. XX 3.4, to prove that `U_{Оұ,K}` and `U_{вҲ’Оұ,K}` do not commute,
which follows at once from 2.11.

It follows from 3.2 and 3.3 that the proof will be complete if we prove:

**Lemma 3.4.** *If `S` is a locally noetherian scheme of dimension `вүӨ 1`, and if `G` is a smooth `S`-group of finite type
with affine connected semisimple fibers, then `G` is affine (and therefore semisimple).*

<!-- label: III.XXV.3.4 -->

**Nota.** In Exp. XVI, it was seen that 3.4 is true without hypothesis on `S`, but the proof is relatively delicate;
since here we need only the particular case 3.4, we give a direct proof of it.

Consider the Lie algebra `g` of `G`, which is a locally free `рқ’Ә_S`-module, and the adjoint representation of `G`

```text
Ad : G вҶ’ GL_{рқ’Ә_S}(g).
```

<!-- original page 278 -->

To prove that `G` is affine over `S`, it suffices to prove that the morphism `Ad` is affine. Since `G` is smooth with
connected fibers, it is separated over `S` (VI_B 5.5), so the morphism `Ad` is separated. Using a result proved in the
appendix (see 4.1), it suffices to prove that the morphism `Ad` is quasi-finite. One is thus reduced to the case where
`S` is the spectrum of a field; in this case `G` is affine, hence semisimple, and one is reduced to Exp. XXII 5.7.14.

## 4. Appendix

<!-- label: III.XXV.4 -->

<!-- original page 278 (cont., page 424 of original) -->

We used in the course of the proof the following proposition:

**Proposition 4.1.** *Let `S` be a locally noetherian scheme of dimension `вүӨ 1`, let `G` and `H` be two `S`-group
schemes of finite type, and `f : G вҶ’ H` a quasi-finite separated group morphism. If `G` is flat over `S`,[^N.D.E-XXV-6]
then `f` is affine.*

<!-- label: III.XXV.4.1 -->

We shall give the proof only in the case where `G` is smooth over `S`, a hypothesis which is indeed verified in the
application we have made of the proposition.

### 4.2.

<!-- label: III.XXV.4.2 -->

By EGA II, 1.6.4, one may suppose `S` reduced. By the usual techniques of passage to the limit,[^N.D.E-XXV-7] one may
suppose `S` local. If `dim(S) = 0`, the assertion is trivial,[^N.D.E-XXV-8] suppose `dim(S) = 1`. By faithfully flat
descent, one may suppose that `S` is complete with algebraically closed residue field. Replacing `S` by its
normalization `SМғ` if necessary, one may (EGA II, 6.7.1 and EGA 0_IV 23.1.5) suppose `S` normal.[^N.D.E-XXV-9] One is
therefore reduced to the case where `S` is the spectrum of a complete discrete valuation ring `A` with algebraically
closed residue field.

### 4.3.

<!-- label: III.XXV.4.3 -->

Let `О·` (resp. `s`) be the generic (resp. closed) point of `S`. Consider the image `f_О·(G_О·)` of `G_О·` in `H_О·`. It is a
closed subgroup scheme of `H_О·`. Let `HвҖІ` be the schematic closure in `H` of `f_О·(G_О·)`. Since `HвҖІ вҶ’ H` is affine (it is
a closed immersion), one may replace `H` by `HвҖІ` and so suppose `H` flat over `S` and `f_О·` surjective. Since `f_О·` is
finite, and `G` and `H` are flat over `S`, one has

```text
dim(G_s) = dim(G_О·) = dim(H_О·) = dim(H_s).
```

### 4.4.

<!-- label: III.XXV.4.4 -->

Let `HвҒ°_s, вҖҰ, HвҒҝ_s` be the irreducible components of `H_s`, where `HвҒ°_s` denotes the neutral component, and let
`z_0, вҖҰ, z_n` be their generic points. Since each local ring `рқ’Ә_{H,z_i}` is of dimension `вүӨ 1`, the morphism
`G Г—_H рқ’Ә_{H,z_i} вҶ’ рқ’Ә_{H,z_i}` is affine because quasi-finite and separated (cf. Exp. XVI, Lemma 4.2), so `G` is affine
over `H` in a neighborhood of `z_i`. Denoting by `V` the largest open subset of `H` such that `G|_V` is affine over `V`,
it follows

<!-- original page 278 (cont., page 425 of original) -->

<!-- original page 279 -->

that `V` contains all the `z_i`,[^N.D.E-XXV-10] so contains at least one closed point `y_i вҲҲ HвҒұ_s` (and one has
`Оә(y_i) = Оә(s)` since `Оә(s)` is algebraically closed).

On the other hand, `V` is obviously stable under the translation defined by any element `g вҲҲ G(S)`. But one has
`dim(G_s) = dim(H_s)` and `f_s` is finite, so

```text
f(s) : GвҒ°_s(s) вҶ’ HвҒ°_s(s)
```

is surjective. Since `A` is complete and `G` smooth over `S`, the canonical map `GвҒ°(S) вҶ’ GвҒ°_s(s)` is surjective; since
`HвҒ°_s(s)` acts transitively on each `HвҒұ_s(s)`, it follows that `V вҠғ H_s(s)`, hence (since `Оә(s)` is algebraically
closed) `V вҠғ H_s`.[^N.D.E-XXV-11]

Since one obviously has `V вҠғ H_О·`, since `f_О·` is finite, one therefore has `V = H`. *QED*.
